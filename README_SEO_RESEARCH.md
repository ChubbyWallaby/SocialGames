## Key Findings (TL;DR)

### 1. Primary Search Behavior
**People don't search for "games" generically.** They search for:
- **Numbered lists** ("60+ games", "50+ games") = people want OPTIONS
- **Alternatives** ("better than truth or dare") = they KNOW classics, want NEW
- **Categories** ("for couples", "for groups", "for remote teams") = context matters
- **Convenience** ("copy and paste", "ready-made") = high intent

### 2. Biggest Opportunity for Confide
**NO ONE owns "async games" or "games without video."**
- Werewolf, Mafia, Imposter Game dominate social deduction search
- But they're all real-time or browser-based
- **Confide's angle:** "Play at your own pace, whenever you want"
- This is a WHITE SPACE in the market

### 3. Pain Point People Have
**Video call fatigue is REAL and growing.**
Exact search phrases:
- "games that don't need video"
- "no video call games"
- "games without being on camera"
- "tired of zoom calls"

### 4. Game Rankings (by search volume)
1. **Truth or Dare** (highest volume, most competitive)
2. **Werewolf** (growing, high competition)
3. **Mafia** (underrated opportunity)
4. **20 Questions** (consistent baseline)
5. **Would You Rather** (growing)
6. **Spyfall** (rising trend 2026)

### 5. Seasonal Patterns
- **No strong seasonal dip** (unlike fitness/shopping)
- **June peak** (summer planning? boredom?)
- **Steady year-round** (people always want to connect)
- **"Timepass" angle evergreen** (people always bored in chats)

## Key Insights for Blog/Product Copy

### Use This Language
✅ "Play at your own pace"
✅ "No scheduling, no video pressure"
✅ "Friends in different time zones? No problem."
✅ "Social games without the camera"
✅ "Async means everyone plays when they want"

### Avoid This Language
❌ "Multiplayer entertainment platform"
❌ "Asynchronous gameplay experience"
❌ "Real-time social deduction engine"
❌ "Text-based interactive gaming"

**Why?** Real people don't search for jargon. They search for problems + benefits.
