# Game 4: The Heist

*Inspired by: Ocean's Eleven, cooperative games with a hidden traitor*

---

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 5-7 |
| **Duration** | 5 days (fixed — tight narrative arc) |
| **Eliminations** | None — everyone plays all 5 days |
| **Confrontation** | Medium |
| **Best for** | Players who like narrative structure, puzzle-solving, and light deduction |
| **Core tension** | "We're all working together... but something keeps going wrong." |

---

## The Concept

The group is a crew planning and executing a fictional heist. Each player has a specialized role (Hacker, Grifter, Driver, etc.). Together you face challenges across 5 days: Reconnaissance → Planning → Preparation → Execution → Aftermath.

But one player — **The Informant** — is secretly an undercover cop. They participate in the heist while subtly sabotaging it from within.

At the end, the group votes on who The Informant is. The outcome (heist success/failure + Informant caught/uncaught) determines winners.

## Setup (Host Instructions)

1. Create WhatsApp group with all players + host
2. Assign crew roles based on player count:

| Players | Roles |
|---------|-------|
| 5 | Mastermind, Hacker, Grifter, Driver, Lookout |
| 6 | + Muscle |
| 7 | + Forger |

3. Secretly assign ONE player as The Informant (they keep their crew role as cover)
4. DM each player their role card (see below)
5. Post welcome message to group

### Role Cards (DM to each player)

**The Mastermind:**
> You see the full picture. Your job: guide the team, keep them focused, and make final calls when the group can't decide. Challenge specialty: coordination and leadership puzzles.

**The Hacker:**
> Tech and logic are your domain. When the challenge involves codes, ciphers, or sequences — you lead. Challenge specialty: logic puzzles.

**The Grifter:**
> You're the smooth talker. Social engineering, cover stories, misdirection. Challenge specialty: writing and bluffing challenges.

**The Driver:**
> Speed and precision under pressure. When time matters, you deliver. Challenge specialty: timed response challenges.

**The Lookout:**
> You notice what others miss. Details, patterns, inconsistencies. Challenge specialty: observation challenges.

**The Muscle (6+ players):**
> Bold action when subtlety fails. You take the risky path. Challenge specialty: high-stakes/bold challenges.

**The Forger (7 players):**
> You create convincing fakes. Documents, identities, diversions. Challenge specialty: creativity challenges.

**The Informant (secret, added to one existing role):**
> You are [Role]. You're also The Informant — an undercover cop embedded in this crew. Your goals: (1) Make the heist fail without being obvious. (2) Survive undetected. Before each phase, I'll DM you a sabotage option. Use it wisely — or don't, if laying low is smarter.

## The 5-Day Structure

---

### Day 1: Reconnaissance

**Post to group:**
> 🔍 **Phase 1: Reconnaissance**
>
> Before you can plan the heist, you need intel on the target.
>
> Each player will receive one piece of intelligence via DM. You MUST share your intel with the group (post it in the chat). Together, you'll piece together the full picture.
>
> After sharing, answer these 3 questions correctly as a group:
> 1. [Question requiring multiple intel pieces]
> 2. [Question requiring multiple intel pieces]
> 3. [Question requiring multiple intel pieces]
>
> Submit your group answers (agree in chat, one person DMs me final answers) by 21:00.
>
> Score: 3 correct = perfect recon. 2 = adequate. 1 or fewer = poor recon (heist gets harder later).

**Example intel fragments:**
- Mastermind receives: "The vault is on the 3rd basement level. Access requires a keycard AND a 6-digit code."
- Hacker receives: "The security system resets every 45 minutes. There's a 90-second window during reset."
- Grifter receives: "The night manager's name is Carvalho. He takes his break at 02:15."
- Driver receives: "The nearest police station is 7 minutes away. Patrol passes every 20 minutes."
- Lookout receives: "There are 4 cameras in the lobby and 2 in the corridor to the vault."
- (If Muscle): "The vault door weighs 3 tons. Emergency lockdown seals it in 30 seconds."

**Example questions:**
1. "How long does the crew have between security reset and the next patrol?" (90 sec window + 20 min patrol = specific calculation)
2. "What's the ideal entry time if the manager's break is at 02:15 and the system resets every 45 min?" (Requires combining multiple facts)
3. "How many security obstacles are between the lobby entrance and the vault?" (Cameras + keycard + code + door)

**Informant sabotage option (DM):**
> You may alter ONE fact in your intel. Change a number, swap a name, or omit a key detail. The group will plan based on wrong information. Choose carefully — if your info is the one that contradicts all others, you'll be suspected.

---

### Day 2: Planning

**Post to group:**
> 📋 **Phase 2: The Plan**
>
> Based on yesterday's intel, it's time to choose your approach.
>
> **Plan A: The Front Door**
> Walk in during operating hours with forged credentials. Fast, bold, high risk.
> Requires: Grifter + Muscle.
> Risk: If recon was poor, 50% chance of immediate detection.
>
> **Plan B: The Underground**
> Access via maintenance tunnels beneath the building. Slow, methodical, low risk.
> Requires: Hacker + Lookout.
> Risk: Takes longer — reduces time in the vault.
>
> **Plan C: The Inside Job**
> Social-engineer the night manager (Carvalho). Medium risk, medium speed.
> Requires: Grifter + Hacker.
> Risk: Manager might not cooperate — depends on Grifter's preparation.
>
> Discuss as a group. Majority vote by 21:00.
> DM me your vote: A, B, or C. Majority wins. Ties = host flips a coin.

**Informant sabotage option (DM):**
> Advocate for the plan that's worst suited to the crew's actual strengths. You don't need to vote against it (suspicious) — just argue for it persuasively in chat. Plant doubt about the better options.

---

### Day 3: Preparation

**Post to group:**
> 🛠️ **Phase 3: Preparation**
>
> The plan is set: [Plan A/B/C].
>
> Each crew member has a preparation task to handle. You'll receive yours via DM. Complete it by 21:00.
>
> Every task completed successfully adds +1 to the Heist Score.
> Target: [4/5/6] out of [5/6/7] tasks must succeed for a clean heist.

**Role-specific tasks (DM individually):**

- **Mastermind:** "Review all 5 intel fragments from Day 1. Identify any inconsistency or error. DM me what doesn't add up (or 'all clear' if it checks out)."
- **Hacker:** "Crack this cipher: [simple substitution cipher or logic puzzle]. DM me the decoded message."
- **Grifter:** "Write a 50-word cover story explaining why you'd be at the bank at 2AM. Must be believable."
- **Driver:** "Speed round: I'll send you 5 yes/no questions over the next 3 hours. Reply within 10 minutes each. Score: correct answers out of 5."
- **Lookout:** "Find 3 specific words I've hidden in the group chat messages from Day 1 and 2 (I'll tell you which words to look for). DM me where they appear."
- **Muscle:** "I'll give you a bold instruction. Post it to the group without context or explanation. (e.g., 'Post the message: The eagle has landed. Say nothing else.')"

**Informant sabotage option (DM):**
> Choose ONE:
> (a) Intentionally fail your own task (but make it look like an honest mistake)
> (b) DM me: "I tip off security" — this makes Day 4 (Execution) harder for whoever takes Stage 3

---

### Day 4: Execution

**Post to group:**
> 🚨 **Phase 4: Execution**
>
> It's go time. The heist unfolds in 3 stages.
>
> Each stage needs ONE crew member to take the lead. Volunteer in the group chat.
> The volunteer receives a challenge via DM. Pass = stage succeeds. Fail = stage fails.
>
> **Stage 1: Entry** — First volunteer steps up.
> **Stage 2: The Vault** — Second volunteer.
> **Stage 3: The Escape** — Third volunteer.
>
> You cannot volunteer for more than one stage.
> If no one volunteers within 2 hours, the Mastermind must assign someone.
>
> Results: 2/3 stages pass = heist succeeds. 1/3 or fewer = heist fails.

**Stage challenges (sent via DM to volunteer):**
- Stage 1 (Entry): "Unscramble this sequence to input the keycard code: [anagram or number puzzle]. You have 2 hours."
- Stage 2 (Vault): "The vault has a timed lock. Answer these 3 questions correctly within 1 hour: [trivia or logic questions]."
- Stage 3 (Escape): "Patrol incoming. Choose the escape route: Route A (fast but exposed) or Route B (slow but hidden). [If Informant tipped off security: Route A automatically fails.]"

**Informant sabotage option (DM):**
> If you volunteer for a stage: you may intentionally provide the wrong answer.
> If you don't volunteer: you may DM me "I alert security" — Stage 3 becomes harder (Route A auto-fails).

---

### Day 5: Aftermath

**Post to group:**
> 🎭 **Phase 5: The Aftermath**
>
> The heist is **[COMPLETE / FAILED]**.
>
> [If success: "The vault is empty. The crew has the loot. But was everyone loyal?"]
> [If failure: "Alarms triggered. The crew scattered. Someone made mistakes — or were they deliberate?"]
>
> **The Question:** Who is The Informant?
>
> Review the last 5 days:
> - Who gave wrong intel?
> - Who argued for a bad plan?
> - Who failed their preparation task?
> - Who volunteered (or avoided volunteering) suspiciously?
>
> **Discuss freely for the next 8 hours.**
> Then DM me your final guess: who is The Informant?
>
> Correct guess = bonus payout.
> The Informant will reveal themselves and explain their moves.

---

## Scoring

| Outcome | Crew Gets | Informant Gets |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Heist succeeds + Informant caught | €10,000 split equally | €0 |
| Heist succeeds + Informant NOT caught | €7,000 split (Informant gets €3,000) | €3,000 |
| Heist fails + Informant caught | €2,000 split (consolation) | €0 |
| Heist fails + Informant NOT caught | €0 | €10,000 |

"Caught" = majority of players correctly identify The Informant in the final vote.

---

## Tips for Hosts

- **The Informant should NOT be the Mastermind.** The Mastermind sees too much — it's too easy to sabotage and too obvious. Hacker or Grifter make the best Informant covers.
- **Make sabotage options genuinely tempting.** If the Informant always lays low, the game is boring. Give them powerful sabotage that creates real consequences.
- **Day 4 is the climax.** Make the Execution challenges feel urgent — short time windows, dramatic narration in the group. "⏰ Security patrol approaching. 45 minutes until detection."
- **Track the Heist Score visually.** After each day, post: "Heist Score: 3/5 ✅. You need 4/5 for a clean job."
- **The Day 5 discussion is everything.** Give it a full 8+ hours. Players will re-read old messages, compare notes, and build cases. Let it breathe.

## 🤖 Bot Variant

This section describes how to run The Heist with an automated bot host. The 5-day narrative structure stays the same, but all challenges become bot-verifiable and the story is delivered through templated messages.

### What Changes

- **Narrative flavor** becomes templated bot messages (pre-written, triggered by game state)
- **All preparation tasks** become bot-verifiable (ciphers, trivia, timed questions — no subjective judgment)
- **Informant sabotage** uses structured choices instead of open-ended instructions
- **Voting** (plan selection + final accusation) is already structured

### Day-by-Day Bot Implementation

#### Day 1: Reconnaissance (Bot Version)

**Bot distributes intel fragments via DM** (same as manual version — no changes needed).

**Bot posts 3 questions to the group.** Players discuss in chat, then ONE player DMs the group's agreed answers.

**Bot logic:** Check each answer against correct answer. Allow numeric tolerance (±5 for numbers, ±1 year for dates). Score: 3/3 = perfect, 2/3 = adequate, 0-1 = poor.

**Informant sabotage (auto-DM):**
> Choose ONE alteration to your intel fragment:
> A) Change a number by 20-50% (e.g., "every 45 minutes" → "every 30 minutes")
> B) Swap a name or detail (e.g., "Carvalho" → "Ferreira")
> C) Omit one specific fact from your fragment
>
> Reply A, B, or C. I'll adjust your fragment before you share it.

**Bot logic:** If Informant picks A, bot modifies the number in their fragment. If B, swaps the name. If C, removes one sentence. The modified fragment is what the Informant shares.

---

#### Day 2: Planning (Bot Version)

No changes needed. Bot posts the three plan options. Collects votes via DM (A, B, or C). Calculates majority. Announces chosen plan.

**Informant sabotage:** No structured sabotage — the Informant just argues in chat. The bot doesn't need to handle this.

---

#### Day 3: Preparation (Bot Version)

All role tasks become bot-verifiable:

| Role | Bot Task |
|------|----------|
| **Mastermind** | "Review all intel fragments. DM me any inconsistency you found, in this format: `[Player]: [what's wrong]`. Or reply `ALL CLEAR`." Bot checks if the identified inconsistency matches the Informant's alteration. |
| **Hacker** | "Decode this cipher: [substitution cipher]. DM me the decoded message." Bot generates a simple cipher (e.g., ROT-13 or letter shift) and checks the answer. |
| **Grifter** | "Answer these 3 social engineering questions: (1) The night manager's name? (2) What time is his break? (3) What's a plausible reason to be at the bank at 2AM? Choose: A) maintenance, B) delivery, C) security audit." Bot checks Q1 and Q2 against stored intel, Q3 is multiple choice. |
| **Driver** | "5 yes/no questions, sent one at a time, 30-minute response window each." Bot sends questions on a timer, checks answers, counts correct responses. |
| **Lookout** | "I've hidden 3 code words in today's group chat messages. Find them and DM me all 3." Bot inserts words into its own scheduled messages (e.g., bolded or capitalized). Checks if player found all 3. |
| **Muscle** | "Choose your approach: A) Force the side entrance, B) Disable the alarm manually, C) Create a distraction. Reply A, B, or C." Bot assigns success probabilities per choice based on the plan chosen on Day 2. |

**Informant sabotage (auto-DM):**
> Choose ONE:
> A) Intentionally fail your task (submit a wrong answer)
> B) Tip off security (makes Day 4 Stage 3 harder — Route A auto-fails)
>
> Reply A or B.

---

#### Day 4: Execution (Bot Version)

**Bot posts:** "Stage 1 needs a volunteer. Reply `VOLUNTEER` within 2 hours."

Bot tracks who volunteers for each stage. Each stage is a timed bot challenge:

| Stage | Bot Challenge |
|-------|--------------|
| **Entry** | Anagram puzzle: unscramble a 6-8 letter word within 2 hours. Bot checks answer. |
| **Vault** | 3 trivia questions, 1-hour deadline. Bot checks all 3. Need 2/3 correct to pass. |
| **Escape** | Choose Route A or Route B. Bot resolves based on game state (if Informant tipped off security, Route A fails). |

**Informant sabotage (auto-DM):**
> If you volunteered: Reply `SABOTAGE` to intentionally fail your stage. Or complete it normally to avoid suspicion.
> If you didn't volunteer: Reply `ALERT` to tip off security (Route A auto-fails for Stage 3).

**Bot logic:** 2/3 stages pass = heist succeeds. Fewer = fails.

---

#### Day 5: Aftermath (Bot Version)

**Bot posts results** (templated based on outcome):
- Heist success: "🏦 The vault is empty. The crew has the loot. But was everyone loyal?"
- Heist failure: "🚨 Alarms triggered. The crew scattered. Someone made mistakes — or were they deliberate?"

**Bot collects final vote:** "DM me: who is The Informant? Reply with one player name."

**Bot scores:**
- Majority correct = Informant caught
- Bot reveals Informant identity
- Bot calculates and posts final payout per the scoring table

### Bot Narrative Templates

The bot sends dramatic messages at key moments. Examples:

- Stage volunteer: "🎯 [Player] steps forward for Stage 1: Entry. The crew watches. No turning back."
- Stage pass: "✅ Stage 1: CLEAR. [Player] cracks the code. The door swings open."
- Stage fail: "❌ Stage 1: FAILED. The keycard is rejected. Alarms begin to chirp."
- Heist result: "📊 Heist Score: [X]/3 stages cleared. The heist [SUCCEEDS/FAILS]."
