# Game 3: The Alliance

*Inspired by: Survivor*

---

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 6-8 (works best with 7-8) |
| **Duration** | 5-7 days (one elimination per day) |
| **Eliminations** | Yes — one player voted out daily |
| **Confrontation** | High |
| **Best for** | Competitive, strategic players who enjoy social manipulation |
| **Core tension** | "Who's actually with me, and who's lying to my face?" |

---

## The Concept

Each day: compete in a challenge for immunity, then vote someone out. Form alliances. Betray them when it serves you. Last player standing faces a jury of everyone they eliminated — and the jury decides the winner.

It's not enough to survive. You have to survive in a way that earns respect.

---

## Setup (Host Instructions)

1. Create WhatsApp group with all players + you (host)
2. Create a second group: "The Jury" (empty at start — eliminated players will be added here)
3. Post welcome message to main group

### Welcome Message (copy-paste to group)

> ⚔️ **Welcome to The Alliance**
>
> Each day has two phases:
> 1. **Challenge** — compete for immunity
> 2. **Tribal Council** — vote someone out
>
> The player who wins the daily challenge CANNOT be voted out that day. Everyone else is fair game.
>
> Eliminated players join **The Jury**. On the final day, the last 2 players standing face The Jury — who votes on the ultimate winner.
>
> **Rules:**
> - Vote via DM to me. Votes are secret until revealed.
> - You cannot vote for yourself or the immunity winner.
> - Discuss, scheme, and ally freely — in the group or in private DMs with each other.
> - Alliances are not binding. Betrayal is legal.
>
> There are [6/7/8] of you. This game runs [5/6/7] days.
> First challenge drops tomorrow at 09:00.
>
> May the best schemer win.

---

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00       — Challenge posted to group (6-8 hour completion window)
15:00-17:00 — Results announced. Immunity winner declared.
17:00-22:00 — Discussion period. Scheme freely. (5-7 hours)
22:00       — Voting deadline. DM host your vote by now.
22:30       — Host reveals votes + announces elimination.
             Eliminated player posts last words, then moves to Jury group.
```

---

## Voting Rules

- DM the host: the name of the player you want eliminated
- You CANNOT vote for yourself
- You CANNOT vote for the immunity winner
- Missing the vote = abstain (counts as no vote, not a penalty, but don't make a habit)
- **Ties:** Host announces a tie. 1-hour sudden-death revote — only non-tied players vote, only between tied players. Second tie = earlier voter's choice breaks it (incentive to vote promptly).

---

## Elimination & Last Words

When eliminated:
- Host posts votes to group: "Votes: [Player A] — 3, [Player B] — 2, [Player C] — 1"
- Eliminated player gets one final message: "Last words — say whatever you want."
- They're then added to The Jury group and muted in the main group.

---

## The Jury (Endgame)

When exactly 2 players remain:

1. Both finalists post a **closing argument** to the main group (visible to all):
   > "Why you should vote for me to win. What was my strategy. Why I deserve this."
2. The Jury (all eliminated players) reads both arguments.
3. Each Jury member DMs host their vote for the winner.
4. Winner is announced.

**Jury deliberation:** Optionally let the Jury discuss among themselves in their private group before voting. Set a 12-hour window.

## Twists (Optional — use 1-2 per game)

**Hidden Immunity Idol:**
On Day 2, host DMs a riddle to the group. First player to solve it and DM the correct answer wins the Idol (secretly — no one else is told). The Idol can be played AFTER votes are revealed: "I play my idol." All votes against them are nullified. Player with next-most votes goes home instead. One-time use, expires Day 5.

**Double Vote:**
The challenge winner on Day 3 also receives (secretly via DM): "You have a Double Vote. On any future tribal council, DM me 'DOUBLE' to make your vote count twice. One use only."

**The Swap:**
On Day 4 (if 5+ players remain), host announces: "Tonight's twist: the player with the MOST votes is NOT eliminated. Instead, they become immune tomorrow. The player with the SECOND most votes goes home." (Rewards being a visible threat.)

**Exile:**
When a player is eliminated, host DMs them: "You've been exiled, but you have one chance to return. Solve this puzzle within 6 hours to re-enter the game." If they solve it, they return with no immunity. If not, they join the Jury as normal.

## Tips for Hosts

- **Reveal full vote breakdowns.** The drama is in seeing who voted for whom. Never hide votes.
- **Let the Jury watch.** Add eliminated players to a spectator mode — they can see the main group but can't type. Or create a separate "Jury Commentary" group where they discuss.
- **Stagger challenge types.** Speed challenges favor attentive players. Creative challenges favor talkers. Prediction challenges favor strategists. Rotate so different players win immunity.
- **The endgame shift.** When 3 players remain, the vibe changes completely. The person who wins final immunity chooses who they sit next to at the Jury. Make this clear — it's the biggest power moment.
- **Track voting patterns.** On Day 4+, post a visual vote history so players can use it strategically:
  > Vote History:
  > Day 1: Ana→Pedro, Pedro→Ana, João→Ana... (Ana eliminated)
  > Day 2: Pedro→Maria, João→Maria... (Maria eliminated)

## 🤖 Bot Variant

This section describes how to run The Alliance with an automated bot host. The bot handles challenge posting, answer verification, immunity calculation, vote collection, and elimination.

### What Changes

- **Dropped challenges** (require human judgment): The Pitch (subjective writing), The Spy Photo (subjective creativity), Two Truths and a Lie (requires knowing players personally)
- **Kept challenges** (bot-verifiable): First to Solve (riddles), The Wager (trivia + risk), The Prediction, The Auction (challenge 7 — sealed bids)
- **New bot-friendly challenges** replace the dropped ones
- **Voting** works identically — already fully structured
- **Ties** handled by bot: automatic revote window, coin flip if still tied

### New Bot-Friendly Challenges

#### Challenge B1: The Blitz

**Bot posts to group:**
> ⚡ **Immunity Challenge: The Blitz**
>
> 10 rapid-fire trivia questions, posted one at a time, 15 minutes apart.
> DM me the answer to each as fast as you can.
>
> Scoring: +1 point per correct answer. Ties broken by total response time.
> Fastest correct player on each question = immunity point.
>
> Highest score = immunity.

**Bot logic:** Post question → start timer → collect DMs → first correct answer = 1 point for that question. After 10 questions, highest total = immunity winner. Ties broken by cumulative response time across correct answers.

**Question bank (host pre-loads):**
- "What is the capital of Australia?" → Canberra
- "How many sides does a hexagon have?" → 6
- "In what year did the Titanic sink?" → 1912
- "What planet is closest to the sun?" → Mercury
- "What is the chemical symbol for iron?" → Fe
- (Expand to 50+ for variety across games)

---

#### Challenge B2: The Number Duel

**Bot posts to group:**
> 🔢 **Immunity Challenge: The Number Duel**
>
> Each player DMs me a number between 1 and 100.
>
> I'll reveal a target number. The player whose number is CLOSEST to the target wins immunity.
>
> Twist: if two players submit the SAME number, they BOTH score 0 (eliminated from this challenge).
>
> Submit within 4 hours.

**Bot logic:** Collect numbers. Generate random target (or use pre-set). Calculate distances. Check for duplicates (both get 0). Closest unique number = immunity.

---

#### Challenge B3: The Accumulator

**Bot posts to group:**
> 📈 **Immunity Challenge: The Accumulator**
>
> 5 estimation questions. For each, DM me your best guess.
>
> Scoring: distance from correct answer is your penalty. LOWEST total penalty = immunity.
>
> Questions:
> 1. "How many islands does Indonesia have?" (Answer: ~17,508)
> 2. "What year was the first email sent?" (Answer: 1971)
> 3. "How many bones does a shark have?" (Answer: 0)
> 4. "How tall is the Statue of Liberty in meters (including pedestal)?" (Answer: 93)
> 5. "How many official languages does South Africa have?" (Answer: 11)
>
> Submit all 5 answers within 6 hours.

**Bot logic:** For each answer, calculate `abs(guess - correct) / correct` as percentage error. Sum across all 5. Lowest total error = immunity.

### Bot Voting System

**After immunity announcement, bot posts:**
> ⚔️ **Tribal Council**
>
> [Immunity winner] is safe. Everyone else is eligible.
>
> DM me the name of the player you want to eliminate.
> Deadline: [timestamp]

**Bot logic:**
1. Collect votes via DM. Validate: can't vote for self, can't vote for immunity holder, must be an active player name.
2. Invalid vote → "❌ Invalid vote. You cannot vote for [reason]. Please resubmit."
3. At deadline: tally votes, announce results.
4. **Tie:** Bot posts "🔄 TIE between [Player A] and [Player B]. Revote — non-tied players only. 1 hour." If still tied: coin flip (random selection).

**Vote reveal format:**
> ⚔️ **Tribal Council Results**
>
> Votes:
> - [Player A]: 3 votes (from: X, Y, Z)
> - [Player B]: 2 votes (from: W, V)
> - [Player C]: 1 vote (from: U)
>
> [Player A] has been eliminated.
> Last words, [Player A]?

### Bot Jury & Endgame

When 2 players remain:

1. Bot DMs both finalists: "Post your closing argument to the group. You have 8 hours."
2. Bot posts arguments (or players post directly).
3. Bot DMs all eliminated (Jury) players: "Vote for the winner: [Player A] or [Player B]."
4. Bot collects jury votes, announces winner.

**Double Vote:** Bot DMs challenge winner on Day 3: "You have a Double Vote. On any future tribal council, reply DOUBLE before submitting your vote to make it count twice."
