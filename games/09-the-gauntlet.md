# Game 9: The Gauntlet

*Inspired by: Ultimate Chicken Horse, Mario Maker*

---

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 5-8 |
| **Duration** | 5 days |
| **Eliminations** | No — everyone plays every round |
| **Confrontation** | Medium |
| **Best for** | Competitive thinkers, trivia fans, anyone who likes building traps for friends |
| **Core tension** | "If I make this too easy, nobody scores. If I make it too hard, I fail my own gauntlet." |

---

## The Concept

Each round, every player builds one **obstacle** — a challenge the whole group must attempt. Then everyone (including the builder) runs the full gauntlet.

Here's the catch:

- If **everyone** clears your obstacle → it was too easy. **No one scores.**
- If **no one** clears your obstacle → it was too hard. **No one scores.**
- Points are awarded **only** when some players clear an obstacle and others don't.

You score for two things: building obstacles that land in the sweet spot, AND clearing obstacles that others couldn't.

The winner isn't the smartest person — it's the person who best reads the group's skill level.

## Obstacle Types

Players choose ONE obstacle type per round. DM it to the host.

### 📝 Trivia
A question with one correct answer. Player sets the question AND the answer.

> Example: "What is the smallest country in South America by area?"
> Answer: Suriname

**Pass condition:** Exact answer (host judges — close enough counts, e.g. "Surinam" = accepted).

### 🧩 Puzzle
A riddle, logic puzzle, or brainteaser.

> Example: "I have cities but no houses, forests but no trees, and water but no fish. What am I?"
> Answer: A map

**Pass condition:** Correct answer.

### 🎯 Estimation
A question where the answer is a number. Player sets the question AND the real answer.

> Example: "How many episodes of The Simpsons have aired?" (Answer: 768)

**Pass condition:** Within 20% of the real answer (host calculates). So for 768, anything from 615 to 921 passes.

### 📸 Photo Challenge
A task requiring players to take or find a photo.

> Example: "Send a photo of something in your home that's older than you."

**Pass condition:** Host judges whether the photo genuinely meets the criteria. Be fair but strict.

### ✍️ Creative
A creative task with a specific constraint.

> Example: "Write a sentence where every word starts with the same letter."

**Pass condition:** Host judges whether the constraint was met.

## Obstacle Rules

**Builders must follow these rules:**

1. **You attempt your own obstacle too.** When the gauntlet is posted, you answer alongside everyone else. (You obviously know the answer to your own — but you still "run" it. This is by design: your obstacle scoring depends on OTHER people's results, not yours.)

2. **Obstacles must be fair.** No personal knowledge ("What's my dog's name?"), no impossible tasks ("Solve this in 2 seconds"), no NSFW content.

3. **Trivia and Estimation must be verifiable.** The host may ask you to provide a source. If challenged and you can't prove your answer is correct, the obstacle is thrown out.

4. **One obstacle per day.** No banking or skipping.

5. **No repeating an obstacle type two days in a row.** If you submitted Trivia yesterday, pick something different today.

## How Anonymous Obstacles Work

When the host posts the daily gauntlet, obstacles are **anonymized** — players don't know who built which one.

> 🏗️ **Today's Gauntlet — 6 Obstacles**
>
> **Obstacle A** (Trivia): "What is the deepest point in the ocean called?"
>
> **Obstacle B** (Estimation): "How many languages are spoken in India?"
>
> **Obstacle C** (Photo): "Send a photo of something purple within arm's reach."
>
> **Obstacle D** (Puzzle): "What word becomes shorter when you add two letters to it?"
>
> **Obstacle E** (Creative): "Write a compliment using exactly 7 words."
>
> **Obstacle F** (Trivia): "Which planet has the most moons?"
>
> **DM me your answers for ALL obstacles by 20:00.**
> Format: "A: [answer], B: [answer], C: [photo], D: [answer], E: [answer], F: [answer]"

After scoring, the host reveals who built each obstacle.

## Scoring

### Builder Points (for your obstacle)

| Result | Builder Gets |
|--------|-------------|
| Everyone passes (too easy) | **0 points** |
| Nobody passes (too hard) | **0 points** |
| 1 person fails, rest pass | **1 point** |
| 2-3 people fail, rest pass | **2 points** (sweet spot) |
| Half fail, half pass (±1) | **3 points** (perfect split) |
| 2-3 people pass, rest fail | **2 points** (sweet spot) |
| 1 person passes, rest fail | **1 point** |

The closer to a 50/50 split, the more the builder scores. A perfect split is the Goldilocks zone.

### Runner Points (for attempting obstacles)

| Result | Runner Gets |
|--------|-------------|
| You pass an obstacle that scored 0 (everyone passed / no one passed) | **0 points** |
| You pass an obstacle that at least 1 other person failed | **1 point** |
| You're the ONLY person who passed an obstacle | **3 points** (hero run) |

## Variants

### 🔥 Escalation Mode
Each day, obstacles must be harder than the previous day (enforced by host judgment). By Day 5, the gauntlet should be brutal.

### 👻 Sabotage Mode
One player is secretly The Saboteur. Their goal: build obstacles that score 0 (too easy or too hard) — they WANT the gauntlet to fail. Everyone else tries to build scoring obstacles. At the end, players vote on who the Saboteur was. If undetected, the Saboteur gets +10 bonus points.

### 🤝 Team Mode (8 players)
Two teams of 4. Each team collectively builds 2 obstacles per day (instead of individual ones). Teams attempt each other's obstacles. Same scoring rules apply, but it's team vs. team.

### ⏱️ Speed Round (single day)
Compress the whole game into one day: 3 rounds of obstacle building + running, 1 hour per round. Good for testing if your group likes the format before committing to 5 days.

## Tips for Hosts

- **Anonymity is key.** Don't reveal who built which obstacle until AFTER scoring. This prevents players from gaming the system by targeting specific builders.
- **Judging creative/photo obstacles is subjective.** Be consistent. If you'd accept it from one player, accept it from all. When in doubt, pass it — the scoring system self-corrects (if you're too lenient, the obstacle scores 0 for being too easy).
- **The 20% estimation window is adjustable.** If your group finds Estimation too easy, tighten to 10%. If too hard, widen to 30%. Announce the threshold on Day 1.
- **Encourage variety.** If everyone submits Trivia every day, the game gets stale. The "no repeats" rule helps, but also praise creative obstacles in your daily results.
- **The "everyone passes" zero is the best teaching moment.** When a builder gets 0 for an easy obstacle, the whole group learns: difficulty is a weapon. It changes behavior fast.
- **Track builder vs. runner points separately.** This creates two leaderboards-within-one. Some players will emerge as great builders (they read the room). Others as great runners (they know things). The overall winner needs both.
- **Day 1 is calibration.** Most players will build too easy on Day 1 (they're being nice). Expect lots of zeroes. By Day 2-3, they'll adjust and the game gets sharp.

## 🤖 Bot Variant

The Gauntlet works with a bot if obstacle types are restricted to those the bot can judge. Photo and free-form Creative challenges are dropped. New bot-friendly types replace them.

### What Changes

- **Dropped obstacle types:** Photo Challenge, Creative (require subjective judgment)
- **Kept obstacle types:** Trivia, Puzzle, Estimation
- **New obstacle types:** Multiple Choice, True/False, Word Count
- **Builder submissions** use a structured format the bot can store and judge
- **Scoring** is fully automated
- **Anonymization** is automatic (bot posts obstacles without builder names)

### Bot Obstacle Types

#### 📝 Trivia (kept)
Builder DMs:
```
TRIVIA
Q: What is the smallest country in South America by area?
A: Suriname
ALT: Surinam
```

Bot logic: Check runner's answer against A and ALT values (case-insensitive, trimmed). Match = pass.

#### 🧩 Puzzle (kept)
Builder DMs:
```
PUZZLE
Q: I have cities but no houses, forests but no trees, water but no fish. What am I?
A: A map
ALT: map
```

Bot logic: Same as Trivia — exact match against answer + alternates.

#### 🎯 Estimation (kept)
Builder DMs:
```
ESTIMATION
Q: How many episodes of The Simpsons have aired?
A: 768
TOLERANCE: 20
```

Bot logic: Runner's answer must be within TOLERANCE% of A. Default 20% if not specified.

#### 🔘 Multiple Choice (new)
Builder DMs:
```
MULTIPLECHOICE
Q: Which of these cities is NOT a national capital?
A: Sydney
B: Tokyo
C: Berlin
D: Ottawa
CORRECT: A
```

Bot logic: Runner replies A/B/C/D. Check against CORRECT.

#### ✅ True/False (new)
Builder DMs:
```
TRUEFALSE
Q: The Great Wall of China is visible from space with the naked eye.
A: FALSE
```

Bot logic: Runner replies TRUE or FALSE. Check against A.

#### 🔢 Word Count (new — replaces Creative)
Builder DMs:
```
WORDCOUNT
Q: Write a sentence about the ocean using exactly 8 words.
WORDS: 8
MUST_CONTAIN: ocean
```

Bot logic: Count words in runner's response. Check if count matches WORDS. If MUST_CONTAIN is set, check that the word appears. Both conditions must be met to pass.

### Bot Obstacle Validation

When a builder submits an obstacle, bot validates:
- Format parseable? If not: "❌ I couldn't parse your obstacle. Use the format: [example for that type]"
- Has question + answer? If not: "❌ Missing answer. Please include A: [answer]"
- Trivia/Estimation answer verifiable? Bot trusts the builder's answer (same as manual version — the builder provides the source if challenged post-game).
- Type repeated from yesterday? "❌ You submitted Trivia yesterday. Choose a different type today."
- Confirmed: "✅ Obstacle received. Type: [type]. It will appear in today's gauntlet."

### Bot Run Phase

At 12:00, bot posts the anonymized gauntlet:

> 🏗️ **Today's Gauntlet — 6 Obstacles**
>
> **A** (Trivia): "What is the deepest point in the ocean called?"
> **B** (Estimation): "How many languages are spoken in India?"
> **C** (Multiple Choice): "Which planet has the shortest day? A) Jupiter B) Saturn C) Mars D) Venus"
> **D** (Puzzle): "What word becomes shorter when you add two letters?"
> **E** (True/False): "Honey never expires. TRUE or FALSE?"
> **F** (Word Count): "Write a sentence about space using exactly 6 words."
>
> DM me your answers by 20:00.
> Format: `A:answer B:answer C:A D:answer E:TRUE F:your sentence`

Bot collects answers, grades each against the stored correct answer, and calculates scoring at 20:00.

### Bot Scoring Automation

Bot calculates builder and runner points automatically using the scoring tables from the main game. For each obstacle:

1. Count passes and fails (excluding the builder — builder always "passes" their own).
2. Look up builder points from the pass/fail split.
3. Award runner points (1 per pass on a scoring obstacle, 3 for hero runs).
4. Post daily results with full breakdown.

**Builder self-pass rule:** The builder always passes their own obstacle (they know the answer). The bot excludes the builder when calculating the pass/fail split for builder points. This means with 6 players, the split is calculated among the 5 non-builders.

**Builder submission (DM to bot):**


**Runner submission (DM to bot):**

- Builder confirmed: "✅ Obstacle received: Trivia. Gauntlet posts at 12:00."
- Runner confirmed: "✅ Answers received for all 6 obstacles."
- Missing answers: "⚠️ You answered 4/6 obstacles. Missing: C, F. Submit by 20:00 or they count as fails."
- Results: Full scored breakdown (same format as manual version).
