# Game 2: Secret Missions

*Inspired by: Secret Story, hidden objective games*

---

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 5-8 |
| **Duration** | 5-7 days |
| **Eliminations** | None — everyone plays the entire game |
| **Confrontation** | Low-Medium |
| **Best for** | Creative groups, actors, people who enjoy wordplay and social manipulation |
| **Core tension** | "Is she doing that naturally or is that her mission?" |

---

## The Concept

Every player receives a secret personal mission they must complete through natural conversation and social interaction in the group chat. Other players try to figure out each other's missions. Complete yours undetected to score. Get caught and lose points.

No one is evil. No one is a traitor. Everyone is simultaneously performing AND detecting.

---

## Setup (Host Instructions)

1. Create WhatsApp group with all players + you (host)
2. Assign each player one mission from the list below (DM privately)
3. Mix difficulties: 2-3 Subtle + 2-3 Moderate + 1-2 Difficult missions per game
4. Post welcome message to group

### Welcome Message (copy-paste to group)

> 🎯 **Welcome to Secret Missions**
>
> Each of you has received a secret mission via DM. Your job: complete it through natural interaction in this group chat — WITHOUT getting caught.
>
> **Scoring:**
> - ✅ Complete your mission undetected → **+10 points**
> - 🟡 Partially complete → **+5 points**
> - 🎯 Correctly accuse another player's exact mission → **+5 points**
> - ❌ Wrong accusation → **-2 points**
> - 🚨 Get correctly accused → **-3 points**
>
> **Accusations:**
> Once per day, you may DM me: "[Player] is doing [specific mission description]."
> I'll reply: "Correct!" or "Wrong."
> The accused is NOT told they were accused (unless it's correct — then they lose points silently).
>
> **Important:** You don't need to accuse. It's a risk/reward choice.
>
> Game ends Day [5/6/7]. All missions revealed. Highest score wins.
>
> Play it cool. Act natural. 🎭

---

## Daily Cycle

```
Morning:  Host posts a conversation prompt (keeps chat alive)
Anytime:  Players interact naturally, working on their missions
Evening:  Host processes accusations received via DM
          Replies to accuser: "Correct! +5" or "Wrong. -2"
Night:    (Optional) Post anonymous stat: "Today: 2 accusations made, 0 correct."
```

## Mission List

Assign one per player. Mix tiers for balance.

### Tier 1: Subtle (Easy to complete, hard to detect)

**1. The Echo**
> Use the exact same phrase another player used (word for word, minimum 4 words) at least 3 times during the game. Must be different phrases from different players.

**2. The Night Owl**
> Send at least one message to the group after midnight on 4 different days.

**3. The Questioner**
> Ask at least 15 genuine questions across the game. (Questions must be real — not rhetorical, not just "?")

**4. The Agreeable**
> Agree with every statement a specific player makes for 3 consecutive days. (Host assigns the target player secretly.)

**5. The Emoji Planter**
> Get 4 different players to use a specific emoji (host assigns, e.g., 💀) at least once each. You cannot use that emoji yourself.

**6. The Storyteller**
> Tell 3 different personal anecdotes (real or fake) across the game. Each must be at least 3 sentences long. No one should question whether they're true.

---

### Tier 2: Moderate (Requires social engineering)

**7. The Rumor Mill**
> Start a false belief that at least 3 players repeat or reference. Must be something invented but plausible. (e.g., "I heard this game concept came from a Danish TV show")

**8. The Puppet Master**
> Get 2 different players to disagree with each other on at least 2 separate occasions. You cannot be directly involved in the disagreement.

**9. The Therapist**
> Get 3 different players to share something personal or emotional (a real opinion, a memory, a vulnerability). Must be genuine, not forced.

**10. The Nickname**
> Introduce a nickname for another player that at least 2 other players use naturally (without you prompting them each time).

**11. The Deflector**
> Get accused of the WRONG mission at least twice. (Perform behaviors that look like other missions without actually completing them.)

**12. The Last Word**
> Be the last person to send a message before the host posts daily results/prompts on at least 4 days.

---

### Tier 3: Difficult (Requires bold moves)

**13. The Liar**
> Tell 5 false statements about yourself that go unquestioned. They must be specific enough that someone COULD challenge them (not "I like food" but "I once lived in Japan for 6 months").

**14. The Prophet**
> Correctly predict 3 things that will happen in the group chat before they happen. DM your prediction to host BEFORE it occurs, then it must come true within 24 hours. (e.g., "I predict Ana will disagree with Pedro today")

**15. The Invisible**
> Be mentioned by name by at least 4 different players in a single day WITHOUT sending any messages yourself that day.

**16. The Chaos Agent**
> Change the topic of conversation at least 5 times successfully. "Successfully" means the group follows your new topic for at least 3 messages.

**17. The Best Friend**
> Get one specific player (host assigns target) to explicitly call you their ally, defend you publicly, or say they trust you.

---

### Tier 4: Expert (High risk, high reward — +15 if undetected)

**18. The Director**
> Get the group to have a conversation that follows a 5-beat structure: introduction → conflict → twist → resolution → callback. DM host your "outline" before attempting. Host judges if it was achieved.

**19. The Mimic**
> Adopt another player's writing style so convincingly that at least one person comments on the similarity or explicitly confuses a message.

**20. The Exit Interview**
> Get every other player to privately DM you at least once during the game (unprompted by you). You may create situations that invite DMs but cannot directly ask.

## Conversation Prompts (Host posts one per day)

These keep the group chat active so missions have material to work with.

1. "What's a skill you're secretly proud of but never bring up?"
2. "Hot take time — share an unpopular opinion you genuinely hold."
3. "If you could swap missions with anyone here (without knowing theirs), who and why?"
4. "Describe your perfect day in exactly 3 sentences."
5. "What's something you've changed your mind about recently?"
6. "React: pineapple on pizza. Defend your position."
7. "Who here do you think has the hardest mission? Why?"
8. "Share a recommendation — book, show, song, anything. Sell it in 2 sentences."
9. "What's the most unusual thing you've done in the last month?"
10. "If this group were a heist crew, what role would each person play?"

## Tips for Hosts

- **Assign missions that suit player personalities.** Give "The Night Owl" to someone who naturally stays up late. Give "The Puppet Master" to someone socially savvy. Give "The Questioner" to someone naturally curious. The fun is making missions that COULD be natural behavior.
- **Don't confirm partial progress.** If a player DMs "Am I done with my mission yet?" — respond "I'll reveal completion status at game end." Keep them guessing.
- **Let accusations drive discussion.** On Day 3-4, post: "Suspicion is rising. Remember: one accusation per day. Choose wisely." This gets people analyzing each other's behavior.
- **Tier 4 missions are for experienced groups only.** First-time groups should use Tiers 1-2 only.
- **The best games happen when prompts are provocative.** Don't post bland questions. The more opinionated the prompt, the more material missions have to work with.
