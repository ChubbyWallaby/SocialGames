# Game 8: The Prophecy

*Inspired by: Mysterium, cooperative escape rooms, puzzle hunts*

---

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 5-7 |

| **Confrontation** | Low |
| **Best for** | Puzzle lovers, cooperative players, people who enjoy mysteries and lateral thinking |

## The Concept

A mystery must be solved. The answer is a specific thing (a place, a person, an event, an object). Each day, every player receives one fragment of the answer via DM and shares it with the group. Together, they piece together the truth.

But one player — **The False Prophet** — receives misleading fragments designed to push the group toward the wrong answer. The False Prophet wins if the group fails.

This is fundamentally a cooperative game with one quiet saboteur.

---

## Setup (Host Instructions)

1. Choose a mystery (answer + clue set) from the examples below, or create your own
2. Create WhatsApp group
3. Assign one player as The False Prophet (DM: "You receive false clues. Your goal: mislead the group without being detected.")
4. Assign all other players as Seekers (DM: "You receive true clues. Work together to solve the prophecy.")
5. Post welcome message

> 🔮 **Welcome to The Prophecy**

>
> Over the next [5/6] days, each of you will receive one **fragment** of the answer via DM each morning. Share your fragment with the group. Combine them. Discuss. Theorize.
>

>

>

> - You MUST share your daily fragment (you can't hoard information)
> - Discuss theories freely in the group
> - The False Prophet participates normally — they share their (false) fragments and discuss theories like everyone else

## How to Design a Mystery

**The formula:**
1. Pick an answer (place, person, event, or thing)
2. Write 5 true clues that progressively narrow down the answer
3. For The False Prophet: keep 2 clues identical to the true ones (cover) and make 3 clues point toward a different-but-plausible wrong answer
4. The best false clues are 80% compatible with the true clues

**Design principle:** Early clues should be broad (multiple things could fit). Later clues should narrow sharply. By Day 4-5, attentive players should be able to solve it — but ONLY if they can identify which fragments are false.

## Creating Your Own Mysteries

### Step-by-step:

1. **Choose an answer.** Best answers are well-known but not immediately obvious from any single clue. Places, famous people, historical events, and iconic objects work best.

2. **Write 5 true clues.** Follow this narrowing pattern:
   - Day 1: Very broad (could be many things)
   - Day 2: Slightly narrower (eliminates some possibilities)
   - Day 3: A distinctive characteristic
   - Day 4: A specific, memorable detail
   - Day 5: Nearly definitive (combined with previous clues, points clearly to the answer)

3. **Choose 2 "cover" days for The False Prophet** (typically Day 1 and Day 3). Give them the same clue as everyone else. This prevents instant detection.

4. **Write 3 false clues** that:
   - Are internally consistent with each other
   - Point toward a different-but-plausible answer
   - Don't OBVIOUSLY contradict the true clues (create ambiguity, not clear conflict)

5. **Test it:** Can the answer be guessed from true clues alone by Day 4? If yes, good. If not, make Day 4 or 5 more specific.

## Tips for Hosts

- **Don't rush.** This game benefits from slow discussion. Let theories develop over hours, not minutes. The 18-24 hour discussion window between fragments is important.
- **Post a "Theory Board" on Day 3.** Ask the group: "What do you think the answer is right now? Top 3 theories." This surfaces disagreement early and helps players identify whose clues are suspicious.
- **The False Prophet should participate actively.** Remind them (via DM): "Join the discussion. Offer theories. Defend your fragments when questioned. Silence is more suspicious than wrong guesses."
- **Cross-referencing is the game.** The magic moment is when a player says "Wait — [Player A]'s Day 3 clue contradicts [Player B]'s Day 4 clue. One of them must be false." Enable this by keeping all fragments visible (pin them or summarize daily).
- **Don't make it too easy.** If the answer is guessable from 2 true clues alone, The False Prophet has no chance. Good mysteries require 4+ true clues combined to be solvable.
- **Harder variation:** Give The False Prophet 3 matching clues and only 2 false ones. This makes detection much harder but the group more likely to solve the puzzle overall.
- **Easier variation:** After Day 3, allow players to submit one "Challenge" — publicly declare "[Player]'s clue is false." Host confirms or denies. One challenge allowed per player, total (use it wisely).

## 🤖 Bot Variant

The Prophecy is fully bot-compatible with zero mechanic changes. The entire game is data-driven: scheduled clue delivery, answer matching, and vote tallying.

### What Changes

- **Mystery data** is loaded as configuration (answer + true clues + false clues + cover days)
- **Clue delivery** is automated on schedule
- **Answer checking** uses fuzzy matching against the correct answer
- **False Prophet vote** is collected and tallied automatically
- **Temperature checks** are scheduled prompts

### Bot Mystery Configuration

Each mystery is stored as a data object:

```
Mystery:
  answer: "Lisbon, Portugal"
  accept: ["lisbon", "lisboa", "lisbon portugal"]
  true_clues:
    day1: "The place sits where a great river meets the sea."
    day2: "Seven hills rise above its white stone streets."
    day3: "A great earthquake destroyed it, and it was reborn in straight lines."
    day4: "Yellow vehicles climb its steepest streets on rails."
    day5: "Explorers departed from here to map the unknown world."
  false_clues:
    day1: "The place sits where a great river meets the sea."
    day2: "Seven hills rise above its ancient stone streets."
    day3: "A great volcanic eruption once threatened its survival."
    day4: "Red double-decker vehicles are its most iconic symbol."
    day5: "Ancient Romans built its first great monuments and roads."
```

Bot loads the mystery config at game start. Multiple mysteries can be pre-loaded and selected randomly or by the game operator.

### Bot Daily Automation

**09:00 — Clue delivery:**
- Bot DMs each Seeker their true clue for that day.
- Bot DMs the False Prophet their false clue for that day.
- Bot posts to group: "🔮 Today's fragments have been sent. Share yours in the group."

**09:30 — Sharing enforcement:**
- Bot tracks who has posted their fragment in the group.
- After 2 hours, bot DMs anyone who hasn't shared: "⏰ You haven't shared today's fragment yet. Please post it in the group."

**18:00 — Temperature check (optional, from Day 2):**
- Bot posts: "🌡️ Temperature Check — What's your current theory? How confident are you (1-10)? Reply in the group."

### Bot Endgame

**Final day, bot posts:**
> 🔮 **The Final Day**
>
> DM me two things:
> 1. Your answer — what is the prophecy about?
> 2. Your accusation — who is The False Prophet? (reply with a player name)
>
> Deadline: 21:00.

**Bot answer matching:**
- Normalize player answer (lowercase, trim whitespace, remove punctuation).
- Check against the `accept` list (exact match on any variant).
- If no exact match: check if the answer CONTAINS any accept variant (e.g., "I think it's Lisbon" → contains "lisbon" → correct).

**Bot scoring:**
- Correct answer: +5 points
- Correct False Prophet identification: +3 bonus
- Wrong answer but correct Prophet: +1 consolation
- False Prophet: group majority wrong → +10 points
- False Prophet: not identified by majority → +5 bonus

**Bot posts final reveal:**
