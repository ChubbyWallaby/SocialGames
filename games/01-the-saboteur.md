# Game 1: The Saboteur

*Inspired by: The Mole (TV show)*

---

## At a Glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 5-8 |
| **Duration** | 5-7 days |
| **Eliminations** | None — everyone plays the entire game |
| **Confrontation** | Low |
| **Best for** | Groups who dislike direct conflict; observation-lovers |
| **Core tension** | "Who just made us fail... on purpose?" |

---

## The Concept

Everyone works together on daily challenges. Each success adds money to a shared pot. Each failure removes money.

One player — **The Saboteur** — is secretly trying to make challenges fail without getting caught.

At the end, every player answers a quiz about The Saboteur's identity and behavior. The player who scores highest on the quiz wins the pot. The Saboteur wins if the pot is empty or if nobody identifies them correctly.

---

## Setup (Host Instructions)

1. Create a WhatsApp group with all players + you (the host)
2. Privately DM each innocent player:
   > "You are a **Team Member**. Work together to pass challenges and observe carefully. At the end, a quiz will test how well you've been watching."
3. Privately DM one player (randomly chosen):
   > "You are **The Saboteur**. Your goal: make the group fail challenges without getting caught. Before each challenge, I'll DM you specific sabotage instructions. Be subtle — obvious sabotage will make you easy to identify in the final quiz."
4. Post the welcome message to the group (see below)

### Welcome Message (copy-paste to group)

> 🎭 **Welcome to The Saboteur**
>
> Over the next [5/6/7] days, you'll face one group challenge per day.
>
> **How it works:**
> - Pass a challenge → money goes IN the pot
> - Fail a challenge → money comes OUT
>
> But one of you is **The Saboteur** — secretly working against the group from the inside.
>
> **How you win:**
> On the final day, everyone answers a quiz about The Saboteur. Who are they? What did they do? When did they strike?
> The player with the highest quiz score wins the pot.
>
> **How The Saboteur wins:**
> If the pot is empty, OR if no one correctly identifies them in the quiz.
>
> **Rules:**
> - Discuss freely in the group
> - All challenge submissions go via DM to me
> - Accuse anyone, anytime — but proof only matters on quiz day
>
> First challenge drops tomorrow at 09:00. 💰 Starting pot: €1,000.

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00  — Host posts challenge to group
09:00-21:00 — Players complete challenge (12-hour window)
21:00  — Host posts results + updated pot total
21:30  — Host privately DMs Saboteur: tomorrow's sabotage instructions
```

---

## The Final Quiz (Last Day)

On the final day, instead of a challenge, DM each player this quiz:

> **Final Quiz — The Saboteur**
>
> Answer each question. Be as specific as possible.
>
> 1. Who is The Saboteur?
> 2. On which day did The Saboteur most clearly sabotage? What did they do?
> 3. Name one day where you think The Saboteur did NOT sabotage (laid low to avoid suspicion).
> 4. What was The Saboteur's overall strategy?
> 5. Rank all other players from most suspicious to least suspicious.

**Scoring:**
- Correct Saboteur identity: +3 points
- Each correct behavioral observation: +1 point
- Host judges "correctness" — partial credit allowed

**Winner:** Highest quiz score takes the pot. Ties split.

## Challenges

Pick 5-7 of these (one per day). They're designed so the Saboteur can subtly interfere without being obvious.

---

### Challenge 1: The Number Game

**Post to group:**
> 🔢 **Challenge: The Number Game**
>
> Each player: DM me a number between 1 and 100. Do NOT discuss your numbers.
>
> If the group average lands between 40 and 60 → pot gains €200.
> Outside that range → pot loses €100.
>
> Submit by 21:00.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Submit either 1 or 100 to pull the average off-target. If questioned afterwards, say you "went with your gut" or "misunderstood and thought we wanted extremes."

---

### Challenge 2: The Photo Chain

**Post to group:**
> 📸 **Challenge: The Photo Chain**
>
> Player order: [list randomized order]
>
> Player 1 receives a word from me. They take a photo related to that word and post it to the group.
> Player 2 takes a photo related to Player 1's photo (not the original word).
> Continue until everyone has posted.
>
> Each player has 2 hours after the previous person posts.
>
> I judge coherence. Clear chain → +€200. Broken chain → -€100.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Post a photo that's technically justifiable but stretches the connection thin. Make it debatable, not obviously wrong. The person after you should struggle.

**Host note:** Start word examples: "warmth", "journey", "sharp", "time"

---

### Challenge 3: The Collective Story

**Post to group:**
> 📖 **Challenge: The Collective Story**
>
> Write a story together. Each player adds exactly ONE sentence, in order.
>
> Rules:
> - No contradicting previous sentences
> - No killing characters others introduced
> - No "and then I woke up" or "it was all a dream"
> - Each sentence must logically follow the last
>
> Order: [randomized list]
> Each person posts their sentence when it's their turn. You have until 21:00 total.
>
> Coherent story → +€200. Incoherent → -€100.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Introduce a confusing element — a new character, a time jump, or a tonal shift. Don't make it nonsensical, just make the next person's job harder. Bonus: if questioned, say "I thought it was more interesting this way."

---

### Challenge 4: The Estimation Game

**Post to group:**
> 🧮 **Challenge: The Estimation Game**
>
> 5 questions below. As a group, agree on ONE answer per question in the group chat. Majority rules.
>
> 1. How many countries are in Africa? (within 5 = correct)
> 2. What year was WhatsApp founded? (within 2 years = correct)
> 3. How tall is the Eiffel Tower in meters? (within 30m = correct)
> 4. How many bones in the adult human body? (within 10 = correct)
> 5. What's the population of Portugal in millions? (within 1M = correct)
>
> Score: 4-5 correct → +€300. 2-3 → +€100. 0-1 → -€200.
>
> Agree and DM me your final answers by 21:00.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Subtly argue for wrong answers. Don't dominate — just "second" other people's wrong guesses or plant doubt on correct ones. Influence 2-3 questions, not all 5. Blend in.

**Answers:** 54 countries, 2009, 330m, 206 bones, ~10.3M

---

### Challenge 5: The Trust Fall

**Post to group:**
> 🤝 **Challenge: The Trust Fall**
>
> Each player: DM me the name of ONE other player you trust the most.
>
> - Mutual trust (A picks B AND B picks A): +€50 per pair
> - One-sided trust: nothing happens
> - If anyone trusts The Saboteur and The Saboteur doesn't trust back: pot loses €100
>
> Choose carefully. DM me by 21:00.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Trust nobody (send me any name — your "trust" won't be reciprocated intentionally). In the group chat, be warm and encouraging to maximize people choosing you. Every player who picks you costs the pot €100.

---

### Challenge 6: The Sequence

**Post to group:**
> 🔄 **Challenge: The Sequence**
>
> I've DM'd each of you a different number (1 through [player count]).
>
> Your task: figure out the correct order from lowest to highest WITHOUT revealing your exact numbers.
>
> Allowed: "I'm in the lower half", "I'm higher than [player]", "I'm not the highest"
> Forbidden: Saying your exact number.
>
> Post your agreed final order in the group chat. Correct → +€300. Wrong → -€150.
>
> You have 8 hours.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Lie about your position. If you're 2, hint you're "middle." If you're 5, say you're "toward the lower end." Create just enough confusion that the group second-guesses. Don't propose the final order yourself.

---

### Challenge 7: The Alibi

**Post to group:**
> 🕵️ **Challenge: The Alibi**
>
> A fictional crime occurred last night at 22:00. Each player: DM me an alibi — where you were, what you were doing, and one specific detail.
>
> I'll post all alibis to the group, **anonymously shuffled**.
> Your task: match each alibi to the correct player.
>
> 5+ correct matches → +€250. 3-4 → +€100. Under 3 → -€150.
>
> Submit alibis by 15:00. Matching phase opens at 16:00, closes 21:00.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Write an alibi that sounds like it could belong to someone else. Mimic another player's known interests or writing style. The goal: create confusion during matching.

---

### Challenge 8: The Budget

**Post to group:**
> 💰 **Challenge: The Budget**
>
> You have a fictional €10,000 to allocate across 5 categories:
> Food, Transport, Entertainment, Health, Education.
>
> Each player DMs me their allocation (must total exactly €10,000).
> NO discussing allocations before submitting.
>
> Score: If all players' allocations are within €1,000 of each other per category → +€300. Otherwise → -€100.
>
> Submit by 21:00.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> Put extreme amounts in 1-2 categories (e.g., €7,000 Entertainment, €500 everything else). This destroys the group's alignment. If questioned: "I just went with what felt right."

---

### Challenge 9: The Whisper Chain

**Post to group:**
> 🗣️ **Challenge: The Whisper Chain**
>
> I'll DM the first player a short sentence. They must DM the NEXT player a paraphrase — same meaning, different words.
>
> Each player paraphrases and passes to the next. Final player posts their version to the group.
>
> If final message still means the same thing → +€250. If meaning is lost → -€100.
>
> Order: [randomized list]
> Each player has 3 hours to paraphrase and pass along.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> When you receive the message, subtly shift the meaning. Don't change it completely — nudge one detail, swap a positive for a negative, or make something ambiguous. The next person should think it still makes sense.

---

### Challenge 10: The Bet

**Post to group:**
> 🎲 **Challenge: The Bet**
>
> I'll state a fact. It is either TRUE or FALSE.
>
> Each player DMs me:
> - Your answer: TRUE or FALSE
> - Your wager: €50, €100, or €200
>
> Correct → wager added to pot. Wrong → wager deducted.
>
> NO GOOGLING. Gut feeling only.
>
> Today's statement: "[insert statement]"
>
> Submit by 21:00.

**Saboteur instructions (DM):**
> In the group chat, confidently advocate for the WRONG answer. Don't personally bet high (suspicious if wrong). Bet €50 on the wrong answer — but convince others to bet €200 on it.

**Statement bank (True/False):**
- "An octopus has three hearts." (TRUE)
- "The Great Wall of China is visible from space with the naked eye." (FALSE)
- "Honey never expires." (TRUE)
- "Lightning never strikes the same place twice." (FALSE)
- "Humans share 50% of their DNA with bananas." (TRUE — it's ~60%)
- "Sound travels faster in water than in air." (TRUE)
- "Vikings wore horned helmets." (FALSE)
- "A goldfish has a 3-second memory." (FALSE)
- "Mount Everest grows approximately 4mm every year." (TRUE)
- "The human nose can detect over 1 trillion scents." (TRUE)

## Pot Tracking

Keep a running total. Post it after every challenge result:

> 📊 **Pot Update**
> Day 1: +€200 (Challenge passed) → Total: €1,200
> Day 2: -€100 (Challenge failed) → Total: €1,100
> Day 3: +€300 (Challenge crushed) → Total: €1,400
> ...

The pot is fictional (bragging rights) unless you decide otherwise.

---

## Tips for Hosts

- **Choose challenges that fit your group.** If players aren't into trivia, skip The Estimation Game. If they love creativity, lean on Photo Chain and Collective Story.
- **Vary challenge types.** Don't do 5 number-based challenges in a row.
- **The Saboteur should succeed sometimes.** If they fail every sabotage, the game is boring. Easy challenges (Number Game, Budget) are almost guaranteed wins for the Saboteur.
- **Post a "Suspicion Corner" prompt on Day 3-4:** "Who's looking suspicious so far? Discuss." This gets the group theorizing mid-game, which makes the final quiz more interesting.
- **Don't reveal results too fast.** Build tension: "Processing results... 🤔" then wait 10-15 minutes before posting.

## 🤖 Bot Variant

This section describes how to run The Saboteur with an automated bot host instead of a human. The bot handles challenge posting, answer collection, scoring, and pot tracking.

### What Changes

- **Dropped challenges** (require human judgment): Photo Chain, Collective Story, The Alibi, Whisper Chain
- **Kept challenges** (bot-verifiable as-is): The Number Game, The Estimation Game, The Trust Fall, The Sequence, The Bet, The Budget
- **New bot-friendly challenges** replace the dropped ones (see below)
- **Saboteur instructions** are pre-programmed per challenge type instead of custom-written by a host
- **Final Quiz** simplified to structured multiple-choice instead of open-ended questions

### New Bot-Friendly Challenges

#### Challenge B1: The Word Chain

**Bot posts to group:**
> 🔗 **Challenge: The Word Chain**
>
> Each player DMs me one word. Words must form a chain: each word starts with the LAST LETTER of the previous word.
>
> Order: [bot-assigned random order]
> Player 1 starts with any word. Each subsequent player sees the previous word and must continue the chain.
>
> Rules: real English words only, no repeats, minimum 4 letters.
>
> Complete chain with no breaks → +€200. Broken chain → -€100.

**Bot logic:** Each player receives the previous player's word via DM. Bot validates: does the new word start with the correct letter? Is it a real word (dictionary lookup)? Are there repeats?

**Saboteur instruction (auto-DM):** "Submit a valid word that makes the NEXT letter extremely difficult (e.g., ending in X, Z, or Q)."

---

#### Challenge B2: The Consensus

**Bot posts to group:**
> 🤝 **Challenge: The Consensus**
>
> I'll give you a category. Each player DMs me ONE answer. If 3+ players give the SAME answer → +€250. If no answer gets 3+ matches → -€100.
>
> Today's category: "[Category]"
>
> Do NOT discuss your answers. Submit by 21:00.

**Category bank:** "A fruit," "A European capital," "A color you'd paint a bedroom," "A movie everyone has seen," "Something you find in a kitchen drawer," "A sport you can play alone," "An animal you'd see at a zoo," "A song everyone knows."

**Bot logic:** Collect all answers, normalize (lowercase, trim), group by exact match, check if any group ≥ 3.

**Saboteur instruction (auto-DM):** "Submit an answer that's technically valid but unlikely to match anyone else. Be creative, not obviously wrong."

---

#### Challenge B3: The Ranking

**Bot posts to group:**
> 📊 **Challenge: The Ranking**
>
> Rank these 5 items from most to least [criterion]. Each player DMs me their ranking privately.
>
> Items: [A, B, C, D, E]
> Criterion: "[e.g., most popular, tallest, oldest, most expensive]"
>
> If the group's average ranking matches the correct order (within 1 swap) → +€200. Otherwise → -€100.

**Example:** "Rank these countries by population, largest to smallest: Brazil, Japan, Nigeria, Germany, Egypt." (Correct: Nigeria, Brazil, Japan, Germany, Egypt — or close.)

**Bot logic:** Each player submits a ranking (e.g., "C, A, E, B, D"). Bot calculates the group's consensus ranking using average position, compares to the factually correct order. Allows ±1 position of tolerance per item.

**Saboteur instruction (auto-DM):** "Submit a ranking with 2-3 items deliberately swapped. Don't invert everything — just enough to pull the average off."

---

#### Challenge B4: The Price Is Right

**Bot posts to group:**
> 💶 **Challenge: The Price Is Right**
>
> How much does this cost?
>
> "[Item description — e.g., 'A one-way Lisbon to London flight (economy, next week)']"
>
> Each player DMs me their estimate. Closest to the actual price wins.
>
> If the group's AVERAGE estimate is within 20% of the real price → +€200. Otherwise → -€100.

**Bot logic:** Collect numeric estimates. Calculate group average. Compare to stored correct answer with 20% tolerance.

**Saboteur instruction (auto-DM):** "Submit an estimate that's 40-60% off the real price — enough to drag the average but not absurdly far."

### Bot Final Quiz

The original quiz is open-ended. The bot version uses structured questions:

**Bot DMs each player:**
> **Final Quiz — The Saboteur**
>
> 1. Who is The Saboteur? (Pick one player name)
> 2. On which day did The Saboteur most clearly sabotage? (Pick a day: 1-5)
> 3. Rank all other players from most suspicious to least suspicious. (List names in order)

**Scoring:**
- Correct Saboteur identity: +3 points
- Correct sabotage day: +1 point
- Saboteur ranked #1 in suspicion list: +1 point

### Bot Message Formats

**Player submissions (DM to bot):**
- Number Game: `42`
- The Bet: `TRUE 200` or `FALSE 50`
- The Budget: `Food:3000 Transport:2000 Entertainment:2000 Health:1500 Education:1500`
- Estimation answers: `54, 2009, 330, 206, 10`
- Trust Fall: `@PlayerName`
- Quiz: `1:Ana 2:Day3 3:Ana,Bruno,Carla,David`

**Bot responses:**
- Invalid format → "❌ I didn't understand that. Please use the format: [example]"
- Submission received → "✅ Got it."
- Deadline passed → "⏰ Time's up. You didn't submit — defaulting to skip."

### Bot Saboteur Assignment

Bot randomly selects one player at game start. Saboteur instructions are sent automatically before each challenge based on challenge type. The saboteur receives a DM with their specific instruction + a reminder: "Be subtle. Obvious sabotage makes you easy to identify."
