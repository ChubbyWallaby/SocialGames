# Social Games Handbook

> A complete guide to hosting WhatsApp-based social games for groups of 5-8 players.
> Fully remote. Fully async. No apps, no downloads, no real-time coordination needed.

---

## How to Use This Handbook

Each game in this document is self-contained. Pick one, read the section, and you can host it tonight.

**You need:**
- A WhatsApp group with 5-8 players
- You (the host) acting as game master via the group + private DMs
- Players who can check their phone **at least twice per day** (morning + evening)

**Async-first design principles used in every game:**
- No action requires real-time presence — players always have multi-hour windows
- Missing a single window is recoverable (graduated penalties, not instant elimination)
- All structured actions (votes, submissions) happen via private DM to the host
- Group chat is for discussion only — no one is penalized for chatting less
- Daily summaries keep slow responders caught up without reading 200 messages

---

## Timing Guidelines (All Games)

| Phase Type | Minimum Window | Recommended | Notes |
|------------|---------------|-------------|-------|
| Submissions (votes, actions) | 6 hours | 8-12 hours | Cover one morning + one evening |
| Discussion | 12 hours | 18-24 hours | People chat when they can |
| Challenge completion | 4 hours | 6-8 hours | Short burst activities |
| Late game (final 2 days) | 4 hours | 6 hours | Compression creates urgency |

**If a player misses a window:**
1. First miss: Host DMs them a reminder. No penalty.
2. Second consecutive miss: Their action defaults (abstain from vote, skip challenge). Group is told "[Player] was absent."
3. Third consecutive miss: Player is removed from game. Their role/position is neutralized cleanly.

# Game 1: The Saboteur

*Inspired by: The Mole (TV show)*

## Overview

Everyone works together on daily challenges. One player — **The Saboteur** — secretly tries to make the group fail. At the end, a quiz determines who was paying the most attention.

**Players:** 5-8
**Duration:** 5-7 days
**Eliminations:** None (everyone plays the entire game)
**Confrontation level:** Low
**Best for:** Groups who dislike direct conflict; observation-lovers

## Setup

1. Create WhatsApp group with all players + you (host)
2. Privately DM each player: "You are a **Team Member**" (innocent)
3. Privately DM one player: "You are **The Saboteur**. Your job: make challenges fail without getting caught."
4. Post to group:

> Welcome to The Saboteur.
>
> Over the next [5-7] days, you'll face one group challenge per day. Every challenge you pass adds money to the pot. Every failure removes money.
>
> But one of you is The Saboteur — working against the group from the inside.
>
> At the end, you'll each answer a quiz about who The Saboteur is and what they did. The player who scores highest on the quiz wins the pot.
>
> The Saboteur wins if the pot is empty OR if no one identifies them correctly.
>
> First challenge drops tomorrow at 9:00.

## The Saboteur's Rules (sent privately)

> You are The Saboteur. Your goals:
> 1. Make challenges fail (or reduce the group's score)
> 2. Do NOT get caught
>
> You will receive secret instructions before each challenge telling you how to sabotage.
> Be subtle. If you're too obvious, players will identify you in the quiz.
>
> You win if:
> - The pot reaches zero, OR
> - No player correctly identifies you in the final quiz

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00  — Host posts challenge to group
09:00-21:00 — Players complete challenge (12-hour window)
21:00  — Host posts results + pot update
21:30  — Host privately sends next-day sabotage instructions to Saboteur
```

## The Final Quiz (Day 7 or last day)

Host DMs each player a quiz:

1. Who is The Saboteur?
2. On which day did The Saboteur most obviously sabotage? What did they do?
3. Name one challenge where The Saboteur did NOT sabotage (tried to lay low).
4. What was The Saboteur's strategy?
5. Rank all players from most suspicious to least suspicious.

**Scoring:** 3 points for correct Saboteur ID, 1 point per correct behavioral observation. Highest score wins.

## Challenges (Pick 5-7 for your game)

### Challenge 1: The Number Game

> **Group Challenge: The Number Game**
>
> Each player must DM me a number between 1 and 100.
> If the group AVERAGE is between 40 and 60, you win €200 for the pot.
> If the average is outside that range, you lose €100.
>
> You have until 21:00 to send your number. No discussing your numbers in the group.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Submit either 1 or 100 to pull the average off-target. If questioned, say you 'misunderstood the rules' or 'went with your gut.'"

---

### Challenge 2: The Photo Chain

> **Group Challenge: The Photo Chain**
>
> I'll send the first player a word. They must take a photo related to that word and post it to the group within 2 hours.
> The next player must then take a photo related to the PREVIOUS photo (not the original word).
> Continue until all players have posted.
>
> I'll judge whether the chain is coherent. Coherent chain = €200. Broken chain = -€100.
>
> Order: [list players in random order]

**Saboteur instruction:** "Post a photo that's technically justifiable but stretches the connection so far it nearly breaks the chain. Don't make it obviously wrong — make it debatable."

---

### Challenge 3: The Collective Story

> **Group Challenge: The Collective Story**
>
> You're writing a story together. Each player adds exactly ONE sentence, in order.
> The story must make logical sense from start to finish.
>
> Rules: No contradicting previous sentences. No killing characters others introduced. No "and then I woke up."
>
> Coherent story = €200. Incoherent = -€100.
>
> Order: [randomized]
> You have until 21:00. Each player sends their sentence to the group when it's their turn.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Add a sentence that introduces a confusing element — a new character, a time jump, or a tonal shift that makes the next person's job harder. Don't make it nonsensical, just... difficult."

---

### Challenge 4: The Estimation Game

> **Group Challenge: The Estimation Game**
>
> I'll ask 5 questions. As a group, you must agree on ONE answer per question (majority vote in the group chat). You have 8 hours.
>
> Questions:
> 1. How many countries are in Africa? (within 5 = correct)
> 2. What year was WhatsApp founded? (within 2 years = correct)
> 3. How tall is the Eiffel Tower in meters? (within 30m = correct)
> 4. How many bones in the adult human body? (within 10 = correct)
> 5. What's the population of Portugal? (within 1 million = correct)
>
> 4-5 correct = €300. 2-3 correct = €100. 0-1 correct = -€200.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Subtly argue for wrong answers. Don't be the loudest voice — just 'second' other people's wrong guesses or plant doubt about correct answers. Pick 2-3 questions to influence, not all 5."

---

### Challenge 5: The Trust Fall

> **Group Challenge: The Trust Fall**
>
> Each player privately DMs me the name of ONE other player they trust most.
> If the trust is MUTUAL (A trusts B AND B trusts A), both earn €50 for the pot.
> If it's one-sided, nothing happens.
> If anyone trusts The Saboteur (and The Saboteur doesn't trust back), the pot LOSES €100.
>
> DM me your choice by 21:00.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Trust no one (send me any name — it won't match). The goal is to have people trust YOU while you betray that trust. In the group chat, be warm and supportive to encourage others to pick you."

---

### Challenge 6: The Sequence

> **Group Challenge: The Sequence**
>
> I'll DM each of you a different number (1 through [number of players]).
> Your task as a group: figure out the order from lowest to highest WITHOUT revealing your exact numbers.
>
> Rules: You may say things like "I'm in the lower half" or "I'm higher than [player]" but you may NOT say your exact number.
>
> Once the group agrees on an order, post it. Correct order = €300. Wrong = -€150.
>
> You have 8 hours.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Lie about your position. If you're number 2, hint that you're 'somewhere in the middle.' Create just enough confusion to make the group second-guess. Don't be the one who proposes the final order."

---

### Challenge 7: The Alibi

> **Group Challenge: The Alibi**
>
> A fictional crime occurred last night at 22:00. Each player must DM me an alibi (where you were, what you were doing, who you were with).
>
> Then I'll post all alibis to the group (anonymously shuffled).
> The group must match each alibi to each player.
>
> 5+ correct matches = €250. 3-4 correct = €100. Under 3 = -€150.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Write an alibi that sounds like it could belong to someone else in the group. Mimic another player's writing style or reference their known interests. The goal: create confusion during the matching phase."

---

### Challenge 8: The Budget

> **Group Challenge: The Budget**
>
> You have a fictional €10,000 to allocate across 5 categories:
> Food, Transport, Entertainment, Health, Education.
>
> Each player DMs me their personal allocation (must total €10,000).
> The GROUP score = how close your answers are to each other. If all allocations are within €1,000 of each other per category, you win €300.
>
> No discussing your allocations before submitting. Submit by 21:00.

**Saboteur instruction:** "Put extreme amounts in one category (e.g., €8,000 on Entertainment, €500 on everything else). This will blow up the group's alignment. If questioned later, say 'I just went with what felt right to me.'"

---

### Challenge 9: The Whisper Chain

> **Group Challenge: The Whisper Chain**
>
> I'll DM the first player a short sentence. They must DM the NEXT player a paraphrase (same meaning, different words). Each player paraphrases and passes it on.
>
> The final player posts what they received to the group.
> If the final message still means the same thing as the original = €250.
> If meaning is lost = -€100.
>
> Order: [randomized list]
> Each person has 3 hours to paraphrase and pass along.

**Saboteur instruction:** "When you receive the message, subtly shift the meaning. Don't change it completely — just nudge a detail, swap a positive for a negative, or make it ambiguous. The person after you should think it still makes sense."

---

### Challenge 10: The Bet

> **Group Challenge: The Bet**
>
> I'll state a fact that is either TRUE or FALSE.
> Each player privately DMs me: TRUE or FALSE + a wager (€50, €100, or €200 from the pot).
>
> Correct answer = your wager is added to pot. Wrong answer = wager is deducted.
>
> Today's statement: "[Insert debatable-sounding fact here]"
>
> Submit by 21:00. No Googling — gut feeling only.

**Saboteur instruction:** "In the group chat, confidently advocate for the WRONG answer. Don't bet the maximum yourself (that would look suspicious if you're wrong). Bet €50 on the wrong answer, but convince others to bet €200 on it."

**Example statements:**
- "An octopus has three hearts." (TRUE)
- "The Great Wall of China is visible from space." (FALSE)
- "Honey never expires." (TRUE)
- "Lightning never strikes the same place twice." (FALSE)
- "Humans use only 10% of their brain." (FALSE)

# Game 2: Secret Missions

*Inspired by: Secret Story, hidden objective games*

## Overview

Every player receives a secret personal mission they must complete through natural conversation and social interaction in the group chat. Other players try to figure out your mission. Complete it undetected to score. Get caught and lose points.

**Players:** 5-8
**Duration:** 5-7 days
**Eliminations:** None
**Confrontation level:** Low-Medium
**Best for:** Creative groups, actors, people who enjoy wordplay

## Setup

1. Create WhatsApp group with all players + host
2. DM each player their secret mission (assign from the mission list below)
3. Post to group:

> Welcome to Secret Missions.
>
> Each of you has received a secret mission via DM. Your job: complete it through natural interaction in this group chat WITHOUT getting caught.
>
> **Scoring:**
> - Complete your mission undetected = +10 points
> - Partially complete = +5 points
> - Get correctly accused by another player = -3 points
> - Correctly accuse another player's mission = +5 points
> - Wrong accusation = -2 points
>
> **Accusations:** Once per day, you may DM me an accusation: "[Player] is doing [specific mission]." I'll confirm or deny.
>
> Game ends on Day [5-7]. All missions revealed. Highest score wins.
>
> Play it cool. Act natural.

## Mission List (assign 1 per player, mix difficulties)

### Tier 1: Subtle (Easy to complete, hard to detect)

1. **The Echo** — Use the exact same phrase another player used (word for word) at least 3 times during the game. Different phrases count.

2. **The Night Owl** — Send at least one message after midnight on 4 different days.

3. **The Questioner** — Ask at least 15 questions total across the game (genuine questions, not just "?").

4. **The Agreeable** — Agree with every single statement another specific player makes for 3 consecutive days (host assigns target player secretly).

5. **The Emoji Planter** — Get 4 different players to use a specific emoji (host assigns emoji, e.g., the skull emoji) at least once each. You cannot use it yourself.

### Tier 2: Moderate (Requires social engineering)

6. **The Rumor Mill** — Start a false belief that at least 3 players repeat or reference. (e.g., "I think this game was inspired by a Portuguese TV show" — something plausible but invented)

7. **The Puppet Master** — Get 2 different players to argue/disagree with each other on at least 2 separate occasions. You cannot be involved in the argument.

8. **The Therapist** — Get 3 different players to share something personal or emotional in the group chat (a real opinion, a memory, a feeling). Must be genuine, not forced.

9. **The Nickname** — Introduce a nickname for another player that at least 2 other people use naturally. The nickname cannot be obviously forced.

10. **The Deflector** — Get accused of the wrong mission at least twice. (Perform behaviors that look like other missions without actually completing them.)

### Tier 3: Difficult (Requires bold moves)

11. **The Liar** — Tell 5 false statements about yourself that go unquestioned (no one challenges them). They must be specific enough that someone COULD challenge them.

12. **The Prophet** — Correctly predict 3 things that will happen in the group chat before they happen. DM your prediction to the host, then it must come true within 24 hours.

13. **The Invisible** — Be mentioned by name by at least 4 different players in a single day WITHOUT sending any messages that day.

14. **The Chaos Agent** — Change the topic of conversation at least 5 times successfully (group follows your new topic for at least 3 messages).

15. **The Best Friend** — Get one specific player (host assigns target) to call you their closest ally, trust you explicitly, or defend you publicly.

### Tier 4: Expert (Near-impossible, high reward)

16. **The Director** — Without anyone noticing, get the group to have a conversation that follows a specific 5-beat narrative structure: introduction → conflict → twist → resolution → callback. DM the host your "script outline" before attempting.

17. **The Mimic** — Adopt another player's writing style so convincingly that at least one person comments on the similarity or confuses a message as being from that player.

18. **The Exit Interview** — Get every player to privately DM you at least once during the game (unprompted by you). You can create situations that encourage DMs but cannot directly ask.

---

## Daily Host Duties

```
Morning:  Post a conversation prompt to keep chat active (see below)
Evening:  Process any accusations received via DM
          Reply to accuser: "Correct! +5 points" or "Wrong. -2 points."
Night:    (Optional) Post a "Daily Suspicion Report" if players want it:
          "Today's accusations: 2 made, 0 correct."
```

## Conversation Prompts (post one per day to keep group chat alive)

1. "Question of the day: What's a skill you're secretly proud of but never mention?"
2. "Hot take time: Share an unpopular opinion you genuinely hold."
3. "If you had to bet on who has the hardest mission here, who would you pick and why?"
4. "Describe your perfect Sunday in exactly 3 sentences."
5. "What's something you've changed your mind about in the last year?"
6. "React to this: pineapple on pizza. Defend your position."
7. "If you could swap missions with anyone here, who would you swap with? (You don't know theirs.)"

# Game 3: The Alliance

*Inspired by: Survivor*

## Overview

Daily challenges + voting. Form alliances. Betray them. Last player standing wins. Eliminated players form a jury that crowns the final winner.

**Players:** 6-8 (works best with 7-8)
**Duration:** 5-7 days (one elimination per day)
**Eliminations:** Yes (one per day)
**Confrontation level:** High
**Best for:** Competitive, strategic players who enjoy social manipulation

## Setup

1. Create WhatsApp group
2. Post to group:

> Welcome to The Alliance.
>
> Each day has two phases:
> 1. **Challenge** — compete for immunity
> 2. **Tribal Council** — vote someone out
>
> The player who wins the daily challenge CANNOT be voted out that day.
> Everyone else is fair game.
>
> Eliminated players join The Jury. On the final day, the last 2 standing face The Jury, who decides the winner.
>
> Alliances will form. Alliances will break. Trust no one completely.
>
> First challenge drops tomorrow at 9:00.

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00  — Challenge posted (6-hour completion window)
15:00  — Results announced. Winner gets immunity.
15:00-22:00 — Discussion period (7 hours). Politick freely.
22:00  — Voting closes. DM host your vote by 22:00.
22:30  — Elimination announced. Eliminated player moves to Jury chat.
```

## Voting Rules

- DM the host the name of the player you want eliminated
- You CANNOT vote for yourself
- You CANNOT vote for the immunity winner
- Ties: Host announces a tie between the two players. A 1-hour sudden-death revote happens (only between tied players, only non-tied players vote)
- Eliminated player may post ONE final message ("last words") before being muted in main group

## The Jury (Endgame)

When 2 players remain:
- Both finalists post a "closing argument" to the group (why they deserve to win)
- All eliminated players (the Jury) DM the host their vote for the winner
- Winner is announced

## Twists (use 1-2 per game to keep it unpredictable)

**Hidden Immunity Idol:**
- On Day 2, host DMs a riddle to the group chat. First player to solve it and DM the answer wins the Idol (secretly).
- Idol can be played AFTER votes are read: "I play my idol." All votes against them don't count. Second-highest vote goes home instead.
- One-time use. Must be played before Day 5.

**Double Vote:**
- Challenge winner on Day 3 receives (secretly via DM): "You have a Double Vote. On any future tribal council, your vote counts twice. DM me 'DOUBLE' when you want to use it."

**The Swap:**
- On Day 4 (if 5+ players remain), host posts: "Twist. The player with the most votes tonight is NOT eliminated. Instead, they become immune tomorrow. The player with the SECOND most votes goes home." (This rewards being a threat.)

---

## Challenges (pick one per day)

### Challenge 1: First to Answer

> **Immunity Challenge: Speed Round**
>
> I'll post 5 riddles over the next 6 hours, one every ~70 minutes.
> First player to DM me the correct answer to each riddle gets 1 point.
> Most points at the end = immunity.
>
> Riddle 1 drops now. Stay alert.

**Example riddles:**
- "I have cities but no houses, forests but no trees, water but no fish. What am I?" (A map)
- "What 5-letter word becomes shorter when you add 2 letters?" (Short → Shorter)
- "I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no body but come alive with wind." (An echo)

---

### Challenge 2: The Wager

> **Immunity Challenge: The Wager**
>
> I'll ask one trivia question. Before I reveal it, each player must DM me a confidence level: LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH.
>
> - LOW = safe but no immunity chance
> - MEDIUM = incorrect answer costs nothing, correct answer earns 2 points
> - HIGH = incorrect answer COSTS 1 point, correct answer earns 5 points
>
> After all wagers are locked, I'll reveal the question. You have 30 minutes to answer.
> Highest points = immunity. Ties broken by speed.

---

### Challenge 3: The Pitch

> **Immunity Challenge: The Pitch**
>
> Convince me. In under 100 words, pitch me on why YOU should win immunity today.
>
> Rules: You may reference other players, your game strategy, or anything you want.
> The group votes on the best pitch (you cannot vote for yourself).
> Most votes = immunity.
>
> DM me your pitch by 15:00. I'll post them all anonymously and the group votes.

---

### Challenge 4: The Spy Photo

> **Immunity Challenge: The Spy Photo**
>
> Take a photo of something in your house that matches this description: "[something blue and smaller than your hand]"
>
> DM it to me within 3 hours. I'll post all photos to the group (anonymously).
> Group votes on the most creative interpretation. Winner = immunity.

---

### Challenge 5: The Prediction

> **Immunity Challenge: The Prediction**
>
> DM me your prediction: Who will receive the most votes at tonight's tribal council?
>
> If you're correct, you automatically win tomorrow's immunity (not today's).
> Today's immunity: the player who sends their prediction FIRST (rewards attentiveness).

---

### Challenge 6: The Lie Detector

> **Immunity Challenge: Two Truths and a Lie**
>
> Each player posts 3 statements about themselves: 2 true, 1 false.
> Everyone DMs me which statement they think is the lie for EACH player.
> Player who fools the most people = immunity.
>
> Post your 3 statements by 13:00. Guesses due by 15:00.

# Game 4: The Heist

*Inspired by: Ocean's Eleven, cooperative games with a traitor*

## Overview

The group plans and executes a fictional heist over 5 days. Each player has a specialized role. One player is secretly **The Informant** (undercover cop) trying to sabotage the heist without being exposed.

**Players:** 5-7
**Duration:** 5 days (fixed, tight)
**Eliminations:** No (but Informant can be "caught" at the end)
**Confrontation level:** Medium
**Best for:** Players who like narrative, puzzle-solving, and light deduction

## Setup

1. Create group. DM roles:
   - **The Mastermind** — sees the full heist plan; guides the team
   - **The Hacker** — solves tech/logic puzzles
   - **The Grifter** — handles social/bluffing challenges
   - **The Driver** — time-sensitive speed challenges
   - **The Lookout** — observes and reports (gets extra information)
   - **The Muscle** — physical/bold challenge handler (if 6+ players)
   - **The Informant** — secretly one of the above roles, ALSO working against the team

2. The Informant receives their cover role (e.g., "You are The Hacker. You're also The Informant.") They participate normally but receive sabotage options each day.

3. Post to group:

> Welcome to The Heist.
>
> Your crew has 5 days to plan and execute a heist on the fictional Meridian Bank.
> Each of you has a role. One of you might not be who they claim.
>
> Each day = one phase of the operation. Succeed at challenges to advance.
> If the heist succeeds: crew splits the loot (minus any damage The Informant caused).
> After the heist: vote on who The Informant is. Correct guess = bonus loot.
>
> Day 1 begins tomorrow: Reconnaissance.

## The 5-Day Structure

### Day 1: Reconnaissance

> **Phase 1: Reconnaissance**
>
> The crew needs intel on the target. Each player receives a different piece of information via DM. Share what you learn with the group to build the full picture.
>
> Challenge: After sharing, answer these 3 questions correctly as a group:
> [Questions that require combining everyone's intel fragments]
>
> Submit group answer by 21:00.

**Informant sabotage option (DM):** "You may alter one detail in your intel fragment. Change a number, swap a name, or omit a key fact. The group will plan based on wrong information."

---

### Day 2: Planning

> **Phase 2: The Plan**
>
> Based on yesterday's intel, choose your approach. Vote as a group:
>
> **Plan A:** Go through the front. High risk, fast execution. Requires Grifter + Muscle.
> **Plan B:** Go underground. Low risk, slow execution. Requires Hacker + Lookout.
> **Plan C:** Social engineering. Medium risk, medium speed. Requires Grifter + Hacker.
>
> Majority vote by 21:00. Ties = host flips a coin.

**Informant sabotage option (DM):** "Advocate strongly for the plan that's worst suited to the group's strengths. You cannot vote against it, but you can argue for it in chat."

---

### Day 3: Preparation

> **Phase 3: Preparation**
>
> Each player gets a role-specific task to complete (DM'd individually):
> - Hacker: Solve a logic puzzle (cipher/code)
> - Grifter: Write a convincing 50-word cover story
> - Driver: Respond to a timed sequence (answer 5 questions within 30 min)
> - Lookout: Find 3 "clues" hidden in previous day's messages (specific words/numbers)
> - Muscle: Complete a boldness challenge (post something specific to group without context)
>
> All tasks due by 21:00. Each completed task = +1 to heist success score.

**Informant sabotage option (DM):** "You may intentionally fail your own task OR DM the host with false information about another player's task ('I noticed [Player] seems confused about their role')."

---

### Day 4: Execution

> **Phase 4: The Heist**
>
> It's go time. The heist unfolds in 3 stages. Each stage requires ONE player to "take the lead" (volunteer in group chat). That player receives a challenge via DM.
>
> Stage 1: Entry — [Puzzle or challenge]
> Stage 2: The Vault — [Puzzle or challenge]
> Stage 3: Escape — [Timed challenge]
>
> If 2/3 stages succeed: Heist succeeds.
> If 1/3 or fewer: Heist fails. Informant wins automatically.

**Informant sabotage option (DM):** "If you volunteer for a stage, you may intentionally provide the wrong answer. If you don't volunteer, you may DM the host: 'I send an anonymous tip to security' — this makes Stage 3 (Escape) harder for whoever takes it."

---

### Day 5: Aftermath

> **Phase 5: The Aftermath**
>
> The heist is [complete/failed].
>
> Now: who is The Informant?
>
> Review the last 5 days. Who made suspicious choices? Who failed their task? Who argued for bad plans?
>
> Each player DMs me their guess. Correct guess = bonus payout.
> The Informant reveals themselves and explains their sabotage.
>
> Final scores calculated. Winner announced.

## Scoring

| Outcome | Crew Payout | Informant Payout |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Heist succeeds, Informant caught | €10,000 split among crew | €0 |
| Heist succeeds, Informant NOT caught | €7,000 split (Informant gets €3,000) | €3,000 |
| Heist fails, Informant caught | €2,000 split (sympathy payout) | €0 |
| Heist fails, Informant NOT caught | €0 | €10,000 |

# Game 5: The Auction

*Inspired by: Trading games, Pit, Monopoly Deal*

## Overview

Players trade resources, fulfill contracts, and accumulate wealth. One player — **The Insider** — has advance knowledge of tomorrow's contracts and uses it for unfair advantage.

**Players:** 5-7
**Duration:** 5 days
**Eliminations:** None
**Confrontation level:** Medium
**Best for:** Analytical players, negotiators, people who enjoy deal-making

## Setup

1. DM each player their starting resources (3 random cards from: Red, Blue, Green, Gold, Silver)
2. DM one player: "You are **The Insider**. Each evening, you'll receive tomorrow's contract 12 hours before everyone else."
3. Post to group:

> Welcome to The Auction.
>
> Each day, a **Contract** is revealed. Contracts pay out if you hold the right combination of resources. Trade with other players to get what you need.
>
> **Rules:**
> - Trading happens in group chat or private DMs (anything goes)
> - Trades are binding once both players confirm to host
> - You may lie about what you hold
> - You may NOT show screenshots of your DMs with the host
>
> One player among you is **The Insider** — they see tomorrow's contract tonight.
> At the end of Day 5, highest total value wins.
>
> Day 1 contract drops at 09:00 tomorrow.

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00  — Contract revealed to group
09:00-20:00 — Trading window (11 hours)
20:00  — Players DM host their current hand
         Host calculates payouts
         (Secretly DM Insider tomorrow's contract)
21:00  — Daily results posted: who earned what (not exact holdings)
         New resource cards distributed (1 random card to each player)
```

## Contracts (one per day)

**Day 1:**
> Contract: "3 of the same color" = €500 payout

**Day 2:**
> Contract: "1 of each: Red + Blue + Green" = €600 payout

**Day 3:**
> Contract: "2 Gold + 1 Silver" = €800 payout

**Day 4:**
> Contract: "Exactly 5 cards of any kind" = €400 per card (€2000 for 5)

**Day 5 (Final):**
> Contract: "The most GOLD cards" = €1500 to whoever holds the most. Ties split.

## Insider Mechanics

- The Insider sees the NEXT day's contract 12 hours early
- This lets them trade for tomorrow's needed resources TODAY (when others don't know why they're valuable)
- The Insider does NOT know what other players hold
- At game end: group votes on who they think The Insider is. Correct guess = Insider loses 50% of earnings. Wrong guess = nothing happens.

## Trading Rules

- Any trade is valid if both players confirm via DM to host
- Trades can be: 1-for-1, 2-for-1, resource-for-favor, or anything creative
- "Futures" are allowed: "I'll give you a Gold tomorrow if you give me 2 Blues today"
- The host tracks all confirmed trades
- Lying about your hand is allowed. Reneging on a confirmed trade is NOT.

# Game 6: The Court

*Inspired by: Political intrigue, Game of Thrones, Diplomacy*

## Overview

Players are nobles in a fictional court. Each has a secret agenda. One player is King each day and can issue decrees. Alliances shift. Assassinations happen. Multiple win conditions mean no one knows who's actually winning.

**Players:** 6-8
**Duration:** 6-7 days
**Eliminations:** Yes (via assassination or overthrow)
**Confrontation level:** Very High
**Best for:** Drama lovers, schemers, people who enjoy complex social dynamics

## Setup

1. Randomly assign starting King (DM them)
2. DM each player a **Secret Agenda** (see list below)
3. Post to group:

> Welcome to The Court.
>
> You are nobles in the Royal Court of [fictional kingdom name].
> One of you is King. The rest plot, scheme, and serve — for now.
>
> **Daily Cycle:**
> - Morning: The King issues a **Decree** (rule change that affects gameplay)
> - Afternoon: Discussion, scheming, alliance-building
> - Evening: Players may attempt **actions** (DM to host): Assassinate, Overthrow, or Protect
>
> **Each player has a secret win condition.** You don't need to survive to win — you need to fulfill your agenda.
>
> The King rules until overthrown. A new King is installed by majority vote.
>
> Crowns are heavy. Daggers are sharp. Trust is expensive.

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00  — King posts their Decree to the group (or DMs host, who posts it)
09:00-20:00 — Discussion, scheming, alliance-building
20:00-22:00 — Action Window: players DM host their nightly action
22:00  — Host resolves all actions. Posts results.
         If King was overthrown: new King announced
         If assassination succeeded: player eliminated
```

## Actions (each player picks ONE per night, DM to host)

- **Assassinate [target]:** 50% chance of success (coin flip). If it fails, YOUR identity as the attacker is revealed to the target only.
- **Protect [target or self]:** Blocks one assassination attempt on that player tonight. You cannot protect yourself two nights in a row.
- **Overthrow:** Only works if 3+ players all submit "Overthrow" on the same night. If threshold met, King is deposed. Overthrowers vote among themselves (DM) for new King.
- **Spy on [target]:** Learn that player's secret agenda keyword (not full agenda, just a hint word).
- **Pass:** Do nothing. Sometimes the safest move.

## The King's Power: Decrees

The King may issue ONE decree per day. Decrees change rules for that day only.

**Example Decrees:**
- "No private DMs today. All communication must be in the group."
- "All actions tonight require 2 players to target the same person to succeed."
- "The player who speaks the least today is immune from assassination tonight."
- "Double votes: whoever overthrows today needs only 2 players, not 3."
- "Silence decree: the first player to speak in the group today loses their action tonight."
- "I pardon [player name] — they cannot be assassinated today."
- "Tax: every player must DM me one piece of information about their agenda. Refusal = suspicion."

## Secret Agendas (assign one per player)

1. **The Survivor:** Win condition: Be alive on the final day. That's it. (Simple but hard when everyone's killing.)
2. **The Kingmaker:** Win condition: The player you installed as King (via overthrow or support) is King on the final day. You cannot be King yourself.
3. **The Assassin:** Win condition: Successfully assassinate 2 specific players (host assigns targets). You don't need to survive.
4. **The Pacifist:** Win condition: No one is assassinated on 3 separate days (not consecutive). Protect actions are your friend.
5. **The Usurper:** Win condition: Become King by Day 5. Hold the crown for 2 consecutive days.
6. **The Spy Master:** Win condition: Correctly identify 3 other players' agendas (via Spy action + deduction). DM host your guesses by Day 6.
7. **The Puppet Master:** Win condition: Get 3 different players to use their action on targets YOU suggested (in group chat or DMs). They don't need to know you're pulling strings.
8. **The Martyr:** Win condition: Get assassinated. Yes, you WANT to die — but you can't make it obvious or people will refuse to waste their action on you.

# Game 7: The Double Life

*Inspired by: The Circle, Catfish*

## Overview

Players create a persona — real or fake. Daily prompts reveal personality. Players rate each other. Top-rated become "Influencers" with power to block others. Catfish who survive win bonus points.

**Players:** 5-8
**Duration:** 5-6 days
**Eliminations:** Yes (1 "blocked" per day by Influencers)
**Confrontation level:** Medium
**Best for:** Extroverts, writers, people who enjoy performing a character

## Setup

1. Before the game starts, DM each player:

> **Profile Setup**
>
> You may play as YOURSELF (real name, real personality) or as a CATFISH (fake persona).
>
> If Catfish: Choose a fake name, fake personality, fake backstory. You'll play the entire game as this character.
>
> DM me:
> - Name (real or fake)
> - 3-sentence bio
> - One profile photo (real selfie OR any image you want to represent your character)
> - Your strategy: Real or Catfish?
>
> No one else will know your choice until the game ends.

2. Host creates profiles and shares them with the group:

> **Meet the players:**
>
> 🟢 [Name 1]: "[Bio]"
> 🟢 [Name 2]: "[Bio]"
> ... etc.
>
> Some may be exactly who they say they are. Some might be... someone else entirely.
>
> First prompt drops tomorrow. Let the games begin.

## Daily Cycle

```
09:00  — Host posts daily prompt (everyone answers in group chat, in character)
09:00-18:00 — Social period: chat, bond, sus each other
18:00  — Rating time: DM host your rating of every player (1-5)
20:00  — Top 2 rated = today's Influencers (announced)
20:00-22:00 — Influencers privately agree on 1 player to BLOCK
22:00  — Blocked player revealed. Their true identity shown. They leave the game.
```

## Rating Criteria (shared with players)

> Rate each player 1-5 based on:
> - How genuine they seem
> - How entertaining/engaging they are
> - How much you'd want them as a real friend
>
> You cannot rate yourself.

## Daily Prompts (one per day)

1. "Tell us about your most embarrassing moment."
2. "Show us a photo of your breakfast/desk/view right now." (Catfish must fake this)
3. "What's your hottest take that might make people dislike you?"
4. "Send a voice note introducing yourself." (Catfish can refuse — but it's suspicious)
5. "Describe your ideal partner in 3 words. Then explain."
6. "If you could only keep one app on your phone, which and why?"
7. "React to this statement: 'Loyalty is more important than honesty.'"

## Endgame (Final Day)

- Last 2-3 players remaining
- Final ratings from ALL players (including eliminated ones, who've been watching)
- Highest-rated overall = Winner
- **Catfish bonus:** If a Catfish player reaches the final AND has an average rating above 3.5, they get bonus points equal to their rating × 2
- **Identity reveal:** All personas revealed. Players react.

# Game 8: The Prophecy

*Inspired by: Cooperative mysteries, escape rooms, Mysterium*

## Overview

A mysterious "prophecy" must be decoded. Each player receives daily clue fragments. Together they solve the overarching puzzle. But one player — **The False Prophet** — receives misleading clues designed to derail the group.

**Players:** 5-7
**Duration:** 5-6 days
**Eliminations:** None
**Confrontation level:** Low
**Best for:** Puzzle lovers, cooperative players, people who enjoy mysteries

## Setup

1. Design a central mystery with an answer (see examples below)
2. Break the solution into fragments — one per player per day
3. Create one set of FALSE fragments for The False Prophet
4. DM each player: "You are a **Seeker**" (innocent) or "You are **The False Prophet**"
5. Post to group:

> Welcome to The Prophecy.
>
> An ancient prophecy foretells something. Over the next [5-6] days, you'll each receive daily fragments of the truth.
>
> Your mission: piece together all fragments and submit the correct answer on the final day.
>
> But beware: one among you receives false visions. Their fragments will lead you astray.
>
> **Rules:**
> - Each morning, you receive a fragment via DM. Share it with the group (or don't — your choice).
> - Discuss, theorize, debate.
> - On the final day, each player submits their answer privately.
> - Correct answer = you win. If group fails, The False Prophet wins.
>
> Day 1 fragment arrives tomorrow at 09:00.

## Example Mystery 1: The Location

**Answer:** Lisbon, Portugal

**True fragments (one per day):**
- Day 1: "The place sits where a great river meets the sea"
- Day 2: "Seven hills rise above its white stone streets"
- Day 3: "A great earthquake destroyed it, and it was reborn in straight lines"
- Day 4: "Yellow vehicles climb its steepest streets on rails"
- Day 5: "Explorers departed from here to map the unknown world"

**False Prophet's fragments:**
- Day 1: "The place sits on an island surrounded by warm waters"
- Day 2: "Seven hills rise above its white stone streets" (this one is true — to maintain cover)
- Day 3: "A volcanic eruption once threatened its survival"
- Day 4: "Red double-decker vehicles are its most famous symbol"
- Day 5: "Ancient Romans built its first great monuments"

## Example Mystery 2: The Person

**Answer:** Marie Curie

**True fragments:**
- Day 1: "Born in a country that did not exist on maps during her childhood"
- Day 2: "She changed the name she was born with when she moved West"
- Day 3: "She won the same great honor twice — no one before had"
- Day 4: "Her work glowed in the dark, and eventually it was what took her life"
- Day 5: "Her personal belongings are still too dangerous to touch without protection"

**False Prophet's fragments:**
- Day 1: "Born in a country that did not exist on maps during her childhood" (true — cover)
- Day 2: "She never married, dedicating her life solely to her work"
- Day 3: "She won the same great honor twice — no one before had" (true — cover)
- Day 4: "Her work was published under a man's name for decades"
- Day 5: "She lived past 90, one of the longest lives in her field"

## Example Mystery 3: The Event

**Answer:** The Moon Landing (1969)

**True fragments:**
- Day 1: "Three people went, but only two touched the surface"
- Day 2: "The whole world watched it happen live, together"
- Day 3: "A flag was placed but it cannot wave"
- Day 4: "It was a race between two nations, and only one arrived"
- Day 5: "A footprint left there will last for millions of years"

**False Prophet's fragments:**
- Day 1: "Three people went, but only two touched the surface" (true — cover)
- Day 2: "It happened in secret, only revealed to the public years later"
- Day 3: "A flag was placed but it cannot wave" (true — cover)
- Day 4: "Many nations collaborated to achieve it together"
- Day 5: "It was repeated successfully dozens of times in the following years"

## How to Create Your Own Mystery

1. Pick an answer (place, person, event, object, concept)
2. Write 5 clues that progressively narrow down the answer
3. For the False Prophet: keep 2 clues true (Days 1 and 3 work best) and make 3 clues point toward a DIFFERENT but plausible answer
4. The best false clues are those that fit 80% of the true clues but point somewhere else

## Scoring

| Outcome | Winners |
|---------|---------|
| All Seekers guess correctly | Seekers win (group victory) |
| Some Seekers correct, some wrong | Correct guessers win individually |
| No one guesses correctly | False Prophet wins |
| Group identifies the False Prophet (bonus vote on final day) | +bonus to group score |

# Hosting Quick Reference

## Before Game Starts (Checklist)

- [ ] Choose a game from this handbook
- [ ] Recruit 5-8 players
- [ ] Create WhatsApp group
- [ ] Decide duration (5 or 7 days)
- [ ] Prepare materials (challenges, clues, missions)
- [ ] Assign roles via DM
- [ ] Post welcome message
- [ ] Set daily alarm for your host duties

## Daily Host Duties (All Games)

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning (09:00) | Post daily challenge/prompt/phase announcement |
| Midday (13:00) | Check for inactive players. Send reminder DM if needed. |
| Evening (20:00-22:00) | Collect submissions. Process votes/actions. Resolve outcomes. |
| Night (22:00-23:00) | Post results/announcements. DM next-day info to special roles. |

## Handling Common Problems

**Player goes silent for 24+ hours:**
> "Hey [name], just checking in. You have [X hours] to submit your [vote/action/answer] or it defaults to [abstain/skip]. Let me know if you need more time!"

**Player wants to quit mid-game:**
> Remove from group gracefully. Their role is neutralized:
> - If they had a hidden role: don't reveal it. Say "[Player] has left for personal reasons."
> - If they were a key role (Saboteur, Informant): secretly reassign to another player, or reduce game length by 1 day.

**Two players have real conflict:**
> DM both privately: "This is a game. If things feel personal, we can pause or adjust. No hard feelings either way." Never address it publicly.

**Game feels slow/dead:**
> Post a provocative prompt or accelerate the timeline: "Due to the slow day, tonight's vote window is extended by 4 hours. Tomorrow's phases will be compressed."
